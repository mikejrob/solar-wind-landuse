                   SENATE BILL NO. 631, SENATE DRAFT 1, HOUSE DRAFT 1
                            RELATING TO RENEWABLE ENERGY

    Chair Tsuji and Members of the Committee:


          Thank you for the opportunity to testify on Senate Bill 631, Senate Draft 1, House
   Draft 1. The Department of Agriculture recognizes that achieving energy security along
   with food security are priorities of the Abercrombie Administration. We support efforts to
   achieve both energy and food security in a manner that is complementary to existing
   law to the greatest extent possible.


          The Department of Agriculture has concerns with this measure as amended,
   particularly with allowing solar as a permissible use on “B” rated agricultural land and on
   designated Important Agricultural Lands (IAL) if the solar facilities are determined to be
   an accessory use to an agricultural-energy facility. Our understanding of Chapter 205 is
   that the primary use of agricultural land is for agricultural production. Section 205-
   2(a)(3) establishes the boundaries of the Agricultural District with the provision that
      .the greatest possible protection shall be given to those lands with a high capacity for
   intensive cultivation.” Section 205-4.5(a), further restricts the use of agricultural lands
   with “A” and “B” rated soils. While Chapter 205 also lists energy-related permissible
   uses including wind energy facilities, biofuel processing facilities, and agricultural-
   energy facilities, we point out that each of these energy-related permissible uses is

required to have a meaningful connection to agriculture, compatibility with agricultural

       We note that Act 231 (SL[1 2008) that established solar energy facilities as a
permissible use within the Agricultural District, purposefully limited this use for “marginal
agricultural lands” with Land Study Bureau “D” and “E” ratings to avoid use of the higher
productivity rated agricultural lands. The Department would not oppose the amendment
that would permit solar energy facilities on “C” rated agricultural land with an acreage
limitation.                                           -

            Chair Tsuji, Vice Chair Hashem, and Members of the Committee.

           The Department of Business, Economic Development, and Tourism (DBEDT) supports

    SB631 SDI, HD1. The bill expands the permitted use of renewable energy on agricultural land.

     Expanding the permitted use of renewable energy on agricultural land helps to ensure we will

    reach the renewable portfolio standard target of 40 perceht renewable generation by 2030.

           This version of the bill includes limits on the amount of solar energy facilities allowed on

    lands rated “B” and “C” as well as prohibits the installation of solar energy facilities in areas

    designated as Important Agricultural Lands. Therefore, it provides a balanced approach to

    preserving the most important agricultural lands while enabling the placement of solar energy

    facilities in some agricultural lands to meet local energy needs.

            Thank you for the opportunity to provide these comments.

               Chair Tsuji, Vice Chair Hashmen and Members of the Committees:

         I give qualified support to SB631 SDI HDI. I appreciate the efforts of the House to
improve this bill since crossover by putting restrictions on use of our best agricultural land for
solar electric production. In the state of HawaU, we have a total 418,701 acres of B and C land.
It is best that we use this valuable land for agricultural purposes because lands that go out of
farming will never get put back into agricultural production.

        Protecting agriculture land has been the number 1 concern in the 1960 Constitution.
Unfortunately, we have failed to resolve this problem. We have lost 50% ofour prime farm
land in the last 50 years. We have to start moving in a different direction in order to save our
agricultural land.

       Please be sure to retain the restriction to scale and appurtenance for solar electrical
geperation on B & C ag land.

       Thank you for this opportunity to testify on an important issue,

Aisha Kadomatsu, Waialae Nui, Honolulu, Hawaii
