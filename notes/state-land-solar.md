# State-Land Solar Leasing in Hawaii: Law, History, Practice, and What Binds

Date: 2026-07-11. Method: four parallel web-research passes (legal framework;
PLDC episode; project inventory; considered-but-not-done), statutes read from
Wayback snapshots of capitol.hawaii.gov hrscurrent pages (capitol is
Cloudflare-blocked to non-browser clients; `web.archive.org/web/<TS>id_/<URL>`
works) plus Justia and the aloha-io HRS mirror. Every claim carries a source;
items not traceable to a document are flagged UNVERIFIED.

Motivating fact (our data, `notes/oahu-ownership.md`): government owns 50.1%
of the Oahu ag district — State 25.8% (incl. DLNR/ADC), federal 19.6%, county
2.7%, DHHL 2.0% — and much of the state share is near-grid, low-soil-class,
and idle.

**Bottom line:** Nothing in Hawaii law *prevents* the state from leasing land
to solar developers. Since 2002/2009 there has been an explicit non-auction,
direct-negotiation pathway (HRS §171-95 + §171-95.3), a 65-year term, and it
has been used — mostly by DHHL, once by UH, prospectively by DLNR (Eurus Olai,
2026) and ADC (Kauai). What is missing is any *program*: no statute, RFP, or
executive order has ever systematically offered DLNR- or ADC-controlled fallow
ag land for utility solar. The binding constraints are political (the 2011–13
PLDC destruction of the state's land-monetization vehicle), institutional
(ADC's ag-only mission, capacity, and conservation-purchase conditions on its
Oahu lands), and procedural (trust obligations and disposition-process
scruples). Statutory prohibition is not among them.

## 1. Legal framework

### 1.1 Chapter 171 default: public auction — with a renewable-energy carve-out

- **Default:** HRS §171-14 — "all disposition of public lands shall be made at
  public auction after public notice as provided in section 171-16" (L 1962,
  c 32). §171-16 requires published notice with TMK, intended use, and upset
  price/rental. [aloha-io mirror §171-14, §171-16]
- **General negotiation exception:** §171-59 — lease by negotiation "upon a
  finding by the board [BLNR] that the public interest demands it," but with
  published criteria and award to highest sealed bid if 2+ qualify; subsection
  (b) fully-negotiated categories (airline/airport, ag processing,
  aquaculture, maritime) do NOT include solar. [aloha-io §171-59]
- **Renewable carve-out, layer 1 — §171-95 (Act 102, SLH 2002):** added
  "renewable energy producers" to the class eligible for **non-auction leases
  and easements up to 65 years**, "at such rental and on such other terms and
  conditions as the board may determine" (§171-95(a)(2)). Definition later
  amended: Act 205 (2007) (thermal/cold-seawater/district cooling), Act 90
  (2008) (biofuel growers), Act 220 (2016), and **Act 53 (SLH 2024, HB 2020)**
  — which dropped the old requirement that the producer sell all net output to
  a regulated utility (now keyed to §269-91 "renewable energy"; no wheeling).
  Verified by diffing 2006/2011/2015/2025 Wayback snapshots of §171-95.
- **Layer 2 — §171-95.3 (Act 19, 2009 Special Session):** "Renewable energy
  producers; lease of public lands without public auction." BLNR "may lease or
  renew a lease... without public auction only pursuant to a public process":
  public notice, opportunity for other producers to participate, "provided
  that nothing in this section shall be construed to prevent the board from
  conducting direct negotiations"; applicant submits timeline, financing plan,
  design, business concept, acreage needs; DBEDT assists evaluation; written
  board report of reasons; **two public hearings on the affected island** and
  board action on that island; no involuntary displacement of a compliant
  existing lessee; right of first refusal on renewal. Never amended as of the
  2024 snapshot. [Wayback: hrscurrent HRS_0171-0095_0003.htm]
  - NOTE: circulating summaries attributing this to "Act 90 (2003)" conflate
    three acts. Act 90 (2003) is the DLNR→DOA/ADC ag-land transfer act
    (38,000+ ac over 20 years); Act 90 (2008) is the biofuel-grower
    definition; the direct-lease process is L Sp 2009 c 19.
  - Rent: §171-95.3 is silent; §171-95(b)(1)'s appraisal floor (§171-17(b))
    textually applies to *public utilities*; whether it binds renewable
    producers is unresolved — UNVERIFIED (no AG opinion or case found).
  - Bill numbers behind Act 19 (Sp 2009) and Act 53 (2024): Act 53 = HB 2020
    (2024, LRB digest); Act 19's bill number UNVERIFIED this session.

### 1.2 Lease term limits

- §171-36(a): general 65-year maximum; extensions under (b) stay within the
  65-year aggregate; no renewable-specific extension. [aloha-io §171-36]
- §171-36.5 (Act 236, SLH 2021, HB 499): BLNR may extend
  commercial/industrial/resort leases up to 40 more years against a
  development agreement for substantial improvements; sunset June 30, 2026
  (extension of sunset UNVERIFIED). Renewables not called out. [Justia;
  Civil Beat 2021-06]

### 1.3 Public land trust / ceded lands / OHA

- Admission Act §5(f) (1959): state lands held in trust for five purposes
  (public education; betterment of native Hawaiians; farm/home ownership;
  public improvements; public use). [LUC "Public Land Trust" summary, June
  2022, luc.hawaii.gov]
- OHA share: HRS §10-13.5 (Act 273, 1980) — 20% of PLT-derived funds. Interim
  fixed sums instead: $15.1M/yr (Act 178, 2006) → **$21.5M/yr + one-time $64M
  (Act 226, SLH 2022)**, CPI-adjusted, plus a PLT-inventory working group.
  2026: SB 903 distributed $55M withheld PLT revenue and revived the working
  group (Star-Advertiser 2026-05-25). Whether renewable-lease rents are inside
  the pro-rata base is exactly what the working group fights over —
  UNVERIFIED pending DLNR receipts reports.
- **Chilling effect — a null finding for leases.** The documented ceded-lands
  chill runs to *sales*: OHA v. HCDCH, 117 Haw. 174 (2008), rev'd, Hawaii v.
  OHA, 556 U.S. 163 (2009), followed by **Act 176 (SLH 2009)** (HRS
  §171-64.7: no sale in fee without 2/3 legislative concurrent resolution).
  No primary document was found showing ceded-lands status has chilled
  *leasing* for renewables; observed conduct cuts the other way (DHHL
  solicits, BLNR leases under §171-95.3). Report as negative finding unless
  docket evidence emerges. (Act 176 (2009) is the land-sale moratorium act;
  it is not a renewable-siting act — corrects our prior.)

### 1.4 DHHL (Hawaiian Homes Commission Act)

- HHCA §204(a)(2): available lands not needed for homesteading may be
  disposed to the public "on the same terms... applicable to the disposition
  of public lands in chapter 171" (post-1985 general leases must carry a
  withdrawal clause; DHHL may negotiate first with native-Hawaiian-controlled
  organizations). §207(a) is the separate 99-yr/$1 homestead lease authority.
  §213(f): receipts go to DHHL's own trust accounts, not the §5(f)/OHA
  stream. [DHHL searchable HHCA PDF]
- DHHL's **Ho'omaluō Energy Policy (2009)** directs pursuit of renewable
  leasing on suitable lands (cited in DHHL 2019-07-11 release). DHHL's Dec.
  2018 disposition notice expressly invokes **§171-95.3 direct negotiation**
  for Kalaeloa (Oahu) and Kahikinui (Maui) parcels. Precise DHHL general-lease
  term cap (65 yr via the ch. 171 incorporation) — plausible, UNVERIFIED as a
  freestanding HHCA provision.

### 1.5 ADC (HRS ch. 163D) — exempt from ch. 171, ag-purposive, no express solar ban

- §163D-4(a)(7),(8),(14): ADC may lease/dispose of real property and develop
  projects with "qualified persons"; **no term limit and no auction
  requirement** in the section.
- **Outside ch. 171:** §163D-16(a) ("Notwithstanding chapter 171
  to the contrary...") and §171-2(11), which excludes ADC-held/set-aside/
  leased lands from the definition of "public lands." So the auction default
  never attaches to ADC dispositions.
- **No express statutory prohibition on non-ag leases.** The constraint is
  purposive: ch. 163D findings/definitions are entirely agricultural, and
  §163D-15.6 directs ADC to secure commitments that central/leeward Oahu ag
  leases "shall not be amended or revoked to allow for a nonagricultural use"
  (55-yr cap on Act 234 (2008) central-Oahu acquisition leases). Whether ADC
  may lease for stand-alone solar is a contested interpretive question. It is not
  a settled ban — no AG opinion or case found (UNVERIFIED either way). In
  practice ADC land **is** hosting solar on Kauai (§3.3, §5.4).

### 1.6 University of Hawaii

- UH lands excluded from "public lands" (§171-2(5)); BOR sets prices/fees for
  "sale, lease, or use of university real property" under §304A-2274 (Act 75,
  2006), "provided that the university shall comply with all statutory and
  common law requirements in the disposition of ceded lands." Used for the
  AES West Oahu project (§3.4).

### 1.7 IAL on state land — a mandate never executed

- Act 183 (2005) created IAL (HRS §§205-41–52); Act 233 (2008) added
  **§205-44.5**: DOA/DLNR to identify public lands for IAL by 2009-12-31 and
  "beginning January 1, 2010... the commission shall designate the public
  lands as important agricultural lands." **Apparently never completed** — no
  LUC adoption of public-land IAL maps found; the one state-land IAL petition
  (Kekaha Agriculture Assn, ~12,123 ac of ADC-managed Kekaha land, LUC docket
  DR21-76) was **withdrawn 2022-06-06**. "No state land has ever been
  designated IAL" is strongly indicated but UNVERIFIED — confirm with LUC.
- Consequence: IAL adds no extra solar friction on state land today (its
  frictions — §205-50(b) DOA/OP referral on SUPs, 2/3 LUC vote to reclassify
  — bind only designated land, which is private). Galbraith's protection
  operates through ownership/deed structure; IAL plays no part.

## 2. The PLDC episode (2011–2013): the state tried to become a land-supplier and politics destroyed the vehicle

- **Creation:** SB 1555 (2011) → **Act 55, SLH 2011**, signed by Gov.
  Abercrombie 2011-05-20 (signing date via secondary sources; effective
  2011-07-01 per bill text); championed by Sens. Donovan Dela Cruz and Malama
  Solomon. Senate vote 23–1 (Ihara no) — UNVERIFIED against the journal.
  Created the Public Land Development Corporation (HRS ch. 171C), attached to
  DLNR, to "make optimal use of public land" and create "revenue-generating
  opportunities," explicitly **modeled on the ADC** (§171C-1). [SB1555 CD1
  via Wayback]
- **The exemption clause (§171C-19, exact text):** projects "shall be exempt
  from all statutes, ordinances, charter provisions, and rules of any
  government agency relating to... land use, zoning, and construction
  standards...," with only "coordination" with county planning required. It
  did not name ch. 205; it was a blanket exemption.
- **Renewable energy was NOT in the enabling act** — zero occurrences of
  "renewable" or "geothermal" in SB1555 CD1 (only "seawater air conditioning
  plants"). A 2012 Dela Cruz proposal would have added geothermal oversight
  and condemnation power (Civil Beat 2012-01); it was proposed but never enacted. Acts
  282 and 309 (SLH 2012) gave PLDC revenue roles tied to Aloha Stadium and
  school lands.
- **It never completed a project** (Star-Advertiser Abercrombie
  retrospective, 2014-11-20). Discussed: harbors, state-park cabins, Aloha
  Stadium lands, workforce housing. ED: Lloyd Haraguchi (ex-James Campbell
  Co.). Early Sunshine Law violations documented.
- **Opposition (revealed conduct, 2012):** statewide rules hearings Aug
  20–31, 2012 drew overflow crowds. Documented opponents on the record:
  Sierra Club Hawaii (dir. Robert Harris) and Moku Loa chapter (Cory Harden);
  OHA (Jocelyn Doane, Nov. 2012 hearing; BOT voted 2013-01-31 to support all
  19 repeal bills — Ka Wai Ola 2013-03); KAHEA/UH's Jonathan Osorio ("the
  state has no business setting up an autonomous entity to make decisions
  about ceded lands"); UNITE HERE Local 5 (Eric Gill); former DLNR chair
  Laura Thielen; Puna's Robert Petricci (tore up the draft rules on camera).
  Testifying in support: Zane & Associates, ex-OEQC dir. Tish Uyehara,
  General Contractors Association (Shannon Alivado). Kauai County Council
  (unanimous) and Hawaii County Council (unanimous, sponsor Brenda Ford)
  passed repeal resolutions; Hawaii State Association of Counties adopted a
  repeal item for 2013. Organizers included Non-Partisan Ohana Hawaii,
  Ko'olauloa Sustainable Communities Committee, Save Oahu Farmlands
  Coalition. Democratic Party of Hawaii repeal resolution: UNVERIFIED.
  [Star-Advertiser 2012-08-30; Big Island Video News 2012-08-22; Maui Now
  2012-11-13; Big Island Now 2012-10-03; Civil Beat 2012-09]
- **Repeal:** **HB 1133 SD2 (2013) → Act 38, SLH 2013**, signed 2013-04-22
  (NOT Act 90; SB 1006 unconfirmed as a PLDC vehicle — corrects our prior).
  Clean repeal of ch. 171C; funds to the land conservation fund/general fund;
  records to DLNR. Abercrombie signing statement: "best intentions and the
  potential for public good could not be reconciled with public concerns."
  Repeal-act preamble: the exemptions "coupled with the manner in which Act
  55 was passed, have led to distrust and uncertainty." Even the original
  sponsors voted for repeal (Environment Hawaii 2013). [HB1133 SD2 via
  Wayback; HRS 171C repeal note "L 2013, c 38, §7"]
- **Aftermath:** Civil Beat's "Land and Power 2023" traces Dela Cruz's
  post-PLDC use of existing quasi-independents (ADC, HTDC) for similar ends
  ("The major PLDC became mini PLDCs" — Choon James). **No document found
  explicitly linking the PLDC episode to state reluctance to lease land for
  solar** — treat that causal link as our hypothesis, UNVERIFIED. But note
  the structural fact: PLDC was modeled on ADC, and ADC is the agency now
  holding the idle central-Oahu ag land.

## 4. Federal precedent on Oahu (brief)

The military has repeatedly supplied land where the state has not:
**Kupono Solar** (Ameresco + Bright Canyon JV) — 42 MW PV + 168 MWh BESS on
131 ac of "underutilized" Navy West Loch Annex land under a **37-year land
lease** (whether formally a 10 U.S.C. §2667 EUL: UNVERIFIED), 20-yr HECO PPA,
commercial ops June 2024. **West Loch Solar** — 20 MW **owned by Hawaiian
Electric** on ~100 ac of Navy land, in service Nov 2019; lease consideration
is **in-kind** (HECO electrical-infrastructure upgrades for the Navy); the
"37-yr outgrant" figure UNVERIFIED. **Schofield Generating Station** — HECO's
50 MW biofuel plant on 8.13 leased Army acres (EIS ROD Feb 2016; in service
2018). **Puuloa Solar** (Ameresco, 6 MW + 30 MWh at JBPHH) selected by HECO
Dec 2023, ops target 2027. On Kauai, **AES PMRF/Kekaha** (14 MW + 70 MWh)
sits on Navy Barking Sands land under an EUL-type arrangement with base
microgrid capability (NREL 2021). Pattern for the rate-base
hypothesis: the two **utility-owned** generation assets (West Loch, Schofield)
are both on federal land — federal landlords deal directly with the utility,
bypassing both ch. 205 and state disposition law entirely. [Sources: Ameresco
/BusinessWire 2022-10-11; hawaiianelectric.com 2024-06-13; Star-Advertiser
2016-07-21; federalregister.gov 2016-02-05; NREL 2021; Civil Beat 2020-12]

## 5. Considered but not done

### 5.1 Authority-building without a program
- Act 102 (2002) and Act 19 (Sp 2009) built the direct-lease authority (§1.1)
  but directed no one to identify or offer parcels. **No statutory state-land
  renewable inventory has ever been published.**
- **Act 207 (SLH 2008)** — HRS ch. 201N "Renewable Energy Facility Siting
  Process": DBEDT permit-coordination (Renewable Energy Facilitator, 6–18 mo
  permit plans). Permitting law, not a land program; developers avoided it
  (NREL/TP-7A20-55630, 2013: skepticism about "losing control of permit
  process decision-making"); **repealed by Act 27, SLH 2016** (repeal-act
  number high-confidence but secondhand). Survivors: DBEDT permitting
  guidebooks and the online Permitting Wizard.
- **HCEI (2008)**: the Oct. 20, 2008 Energy Agreement (full text on SEC
  EDGAR) contains **no state-land leasing commitment**; the state's only
  siting undertaking was lead responsibility on the interisland cable.
- HSEO's **Renewable EnerGIS** tool layers resource data over government
  ownership and soil class — a screen, not a program.

### 5.2 Bills 2014–2026 — a null result
No bill found, passed or failed, establishing a state-land solar leasing
program or directing DLNR/ADC to offer fallow ag land for solar (caveat:
capitol full-text search was inaccessible; re-check when Phase-1 scraping
resumes). Edge cases: Act 53 (2024, HB 2020) quietly widened §171-95
eligibility; **SB 91 (2023–24)** (OPSD statewide agrivoltaics study) died in
WAM; **HB 778 (2025), enacted** — OPSD integrated land use study of the ag
districts reconciling housing/food/RPS targets, interim report due before the
2026 session, final before 2027 — is the first official state screen that
could put state parcels on the solar map. 2026: SB 2169 gave ADC eminent
domain for ag purposes (passed May 2026, 11 House noes); SB 2595 (ADC
market-rent mandate) went to conference, disposition UNVERIFIED. A 2026
Hawaii agrivoltaics bill is referenced in national coverage (Pluribus News
2026-05-12) but its number is UNVERIFIED. (Our candidate numbers SB 2988 /
HB 1590 checked out as unrelated bills.)

### 5.3 Why not ADC's Oahu lands (Galbraith/Whitmore)?
- **Galbraith acquisition (closed 2012-12-10/12):** TPL bought 1,743 ac of
  the George Galbraith Trust estate for $25M; ~1,207 ac to ADC, ~511 ac to
  OHA (Kūkaniloko buffer). Funding: $13M state GO bonds, $4.5M U.S. Army
  (compatible-use buffer), $4M C&C Honolulu Clean Water & Natural Lands Fund,
  $3M OHA, $0.5M D.R. Horton–Schuler. TPL: "The properties can only be used
  for agriculture." **Our USDA-FRPP-easement hypothesis is NOT SUPPORTED** —
  no FRPP or Legacy Land money in the stack; the restriction inference runs
  through the Army and CWNL funding conditions plus the TPL statement. The
  recorded easement instruments were not located online — pull the 2012
  Bureau of Conveyances documents; scope of deed restrictions =
  documented-but-instrument-unverified.
- **Whitmore:** ADC bought 257 ac from Dole 2015-02-26 for $5.6M ($2.2M over
  the initial appraisal); Environment Hawaii documents encroachments and
  stalled irrigation, **no mention of solar**.
- **Audit Report 21-01 (Jan 2021)**, full text: 7 of 12 ADC Central Oahu
  properties completely vacant — "more than 1,700 acres of ADC's Central Oahu
  land sitting idle"; statewide vacancy >51%; "ADC was never intended to
  manage a portfolio of vacant lands"; records "not auditable." **The audit
  never mentions solar or renewable energy (zero grep hits)** — even the
  auditor framed idle land purely as an ag problem.
- No ADC board minutes, news, or proposals for solar/agrivoltaics on
  Galbraith/Whitmore surfaced; the "2022+ ADC agrivoltaics pilot" lead is
  UNVERIFIED/not found (the visible agrivoltaics trial is HARC's, on its own
  Kunia Research Station land). Counterpoint: ADC land on Kauai (Kekaha) is
  hosting WKEP's planned ~375-ac array and Mana Solar — so ADC land is not
  categorically closed to solar; the Oahu lands specifically are bound by
  conservation-purchase conditions, the ag-hub program, and (per the audit)
  administrative incapacity.

### 5.4 Considered-and-stopped: the HCDA process cases (2017)
- March 2017: HCDA board authorized lease negotiations with Aloha Solar
  Energy Fund II (ECC) for 5 MW on ~24 ac near Kalaeloa Airport.
- **2017-09-06: the Kalaeloa Authority board voted 7–2 to REFUSE lease
  negotiations with SunStrong II LLC** (5 MW, state Parcel 13073-C, 19.36 ac)
  — explicitly because staff had run no RFI/public competition: "not based on
  the merits of the project but the process" (Chair John Whalen,
  Star-Advertiser 2017-09-07). Cleanest documented case of state-land solar
  stopped, and the stated reason is disposition process, not agriculture.

### 5.5 Executive/utility screens now underway
- Gov. Green EO 25-01 (2025-01-29) accelerates RE targets and permitting but
  contains no state-land leasing directive (per HSEO summary; full EO not
  fetched). HECO's Stage 3 RFP (7 projects selected 2023-12-01) contained no

- **HECO Renewable Energy Zones (announced 2026-07-08):** prioritizes Zone 1
  Kunia/Schofield and Zone 6 Ko'olaupoko; "limited land and many competing
  land use needs remain a primary challenge"; **"workshops also were held
  with state and federal government officials to identify government-held
  land that could possibly be used for renewable generation projects"**; next
  steps track the HB 778 study. First systematic state-land screen for solar,
  happening now — Kunia/Schofield is the same central-Oahu belt as the ADC
  Galbraith lands.
- Context flag: the June 2026 UHERO/Civil Beat analysis of Oahu solar land
  availability (27,500 ac now vs ~31,500 ac needed; 49,000 ac if restrictions
  ease) is Roberts/Hartley/Fripp — per Civil Beat (2026-07) the paper was
  withdrawn; cite with care in the political-economy paper.

## 6. Assessment: what binds state-land solar leasing

1. **The statute does not bind.** Since 2002/2009 the law affirmatively *enables*
   direct-negotiated 65-year renewable leases (§171-95, §171-95.3), and Act
   53 (2024) loosened it further. ADC is exempt from auction rules entirely.
   Ch. 205's ag-land solar cap still applies to state ag-district land that
   is not federally held (the UH West Oahu project needed an LUC SUP), but
   the operating state-land projects sit on urban-district land (Kalaeloa,
   Campbell Industrial Park) — the ch. 205 regime channels state-land solar
   toward the small urban-district stock exactly as it does private land.
2. **Trust politics, but subtler than "ceded lands block it."** The
   documented ceded-lands chill applies to *sales* (2008–09 litigation, Act 176
   (2009) 2/3-vote requirement) — a null finding for leases. The live trust
   frictions are distributional: the OHA 20%/interim-sum fight (Act 226
   (2022), SB 903 (2026)) makes every new revenue stream contested
   accounting, and DHHL leasing is disciplined by beneficiary politics
   (Nanakuli 2021 opposition stalled a selected project).
3. **The PLDC scar.** The one entity built to monetize idle state land was
   destroyed within two years by a broad coalition (Sierra Club, OHA,
   Hawaiian organizations, Local 5, counties) — over its regulatory
   exemptions and process; renewables weren't in the act.
   No document explicitly ties the episode to later solar reluctance
   (UNVERIFIED as causation), but no successor vehicle has been attempted,
   and post-PLDC land development migrated into low-profile
   quasi-independents (Civil Beat "Land and Power 2023").
4. **Agency mission and capacity.** ADC — holder of the idle central-Oahu ag
   land — has an ag-only purposive mandate, conservation-funded acquisition
   conditions on Galbraith, and, per Audit 21-01, could not manage the
   portfolio it has; solar appears nowhere in its record on Oahu. DLNR runs
   no proactive program (its one Oahu solar disposition, Eurus Olai, was
   developer-initiated). Process scruples bind even willing agencies (HCDA
   2017 refusal for want of an RFI).
5. **Net: inertia plus fragmentation, defended by trust politics.** Each
   agency can lease; none is tasked to; no one owns the question. The
   plausible unlock, on this record: (a) an inventory-plus-offer mandate —
   HB 778's integrated land use study and HECO's 2026 REZ
   government-land workshops are the first such screens; (b) an executed
   §205-44.5-style designation sorting state ag land into keep-for-ag vs
   lease-for-energy; (c) a disposition template that pre-commits revenue
   shares (OHA/DHHL beneficiaries) the way the Anahola lease's community
   benefits agreement did — the one state-land model that got built with
   beneficiary consent. A reform that merely re-created a PLDC-style exempt
   developer would likely re-run 2012–13.
