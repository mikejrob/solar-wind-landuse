# Sierra Club Hawai'i Chapter, 2009–2014: people, the ag-land campaign, and the food-security frame

Research note, 2026-07-11. Question: was the chapter's 2011 opposition to SB 631 (solar
on ag land) a food-policy position per se, or the legislative arm of an ag-district-integrity
/ anti-sprawl commitment strategy anchored in the Koa Ridge and Ho'opili fights?

Evidentiary basis: Wayback Machine captures of sierraclubhawaii.com (2010–2012), court
opinions, LUC docket materials, contemporaneous press. capitol.hawaii.gov and
data.capitol.hawaii.gov both return 403 to direct fetches, so 2011/2014 testimony PDFs
could not be re-pulled here; SB 631 testimony content below is as verified previously by
the project. Claims tagged UNVERIFIED need a primary source before use in the paper.

## 2. Land-use campaign record, 2008–2014

**Turtle Bay / Kuilima expansion (2006–2010).** Sierra Club Hawai'i Chapter was
co-plaintiff with Keep the North Shore Country (suit filed 2006-05-19; companion to Unite
Here! Local 5's case). Hawai'i Supreme Court, 2010-04-08 (*Unite Here! Local 5 v. City &
County of Honolulu*): the 1985 EIS was stale; supplemental EIS required. Landmark
precedent that EISs don't last forever.
Sources: [Surfrider O'ahu campaign page](https://oahu.surfrider.org/campaigns/Turtle+Bay+Kuilima+Resort+Required+to+do+SEIS); [Civil Beat 2010-04](https://www.civilbeat.org/2010/04/448-implications-of-turtle-bay-ruling-are-both-narrow-and-wide/); [FindLaw, Unite Here v. C&C Honolulu (2010)](https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/hi-supreme-court/1521006.html).

**Koa Ridge (Castle & Cooke; ~768 ac prime ag land, Waipi'o/Waiawa, Central O'ahu).**
Sierra Club (with Sen. Clayton Hee and Richard Poirier) contested reclassification at the
LUC "largely over the loss of prime farmland, which they contended was necessary for the
state to become more self-sufficient in its food supply" ([Civil Beat 2014-02](https://www.civilbeat.org/2014/02/hawaii-supreme-court-takes-up-lawsuit-challenging-koa/)). An earlier
round (EA requirement) was lost in *Sierra Club v. Office of Planning* (2006). LUC approved
Oct 2010; the Club challenged the vote in circuit court (Dec 2010, [Hawaii News Now](https://www.hawaiinewsnow.com/story/13495025/sierra-club-challenging-koa-ridge-vote/)) and its
2011 homepage celebrated "a legal victory in the Koa Ridge matter" (Wayback 2011-12-04).
After reapproval, Sierra Club & Hee appealed: Civ. No. 12-1-1999 → **SCAP-13-0000765,
memorandum opinion 2016-04-06, affirmed (Pollack, J., dissenting)**. Arguments: Haw.
Const. art. XI §3 (conserve ag lands), Act 183 (2005) IAL designation should precede
reclassification, and HAR §15-15-77(b)(6) ("shall not be taken out of the agricultural
district unless... will not substantially impair actual or potential agricultural
production... or is reasonably necessary for urban growth"). Even Castle & Cooke's expert
conceded Koa Ridge would take ~5% of O'ahu's remaining prime (ALISH A/B) farmland.
Source: [SCAP-13-0000765 mem. op.](https://www.courts.state.hi.us/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/SCAP-13-0000765mop.pdf) (pp. 1–3 read directly). LUC docket number: the LUC O'ahu
completed-dockets list shows Castle & Cooke dockets A00-734, A07-775, A11-793; which is
Koa Ridge is UNVERIFIED (likely A11-793 for the refiled petition — confirm at luc.hawaii.gov).

**Ho'opili (D.R. Horton-Schuler; ~1,525 ac, 'Ewa).** LUC Docket **A06-771**. Sierra Club
intervened 2011 (its homepage that fall urged members to email the LUC, "Refer to Docket
No. A06-771"); represented at the LUC by **Elizabeth Dunne, Esq.** ([LUC minutes 2012-03-16](https://files.hawaii.gov/luc/meetings/minutes/2012/03162012honolulu.pdf)). LUC approved
June 2012. Appeal: *Sierra Club v. D.R. Horton-Schuler Homes* , **SCAP-13-0002266, decided
2015-12-22, affirmed, Pollack, J. dissenting**; Sierra Club counsel on appeal **Eric A.
Seitz, Della A. Belatti, Sarah R. Devine** (private firm). Same art. XI §3 / IAL-first
arguments; Club emphasized the lands were "among the most productive and valuable lands in
the state." In 2015 the Club joined Friends of Makakilo's motion for an order to show
cause ([LUC filing 2015-08](https://luc.hawaii.gov/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/A06-771-sierra-club-joinder-to-FOM-MTN.pdf)).
Sources: [FindLaw](https://caselaw.findlaw.com/hi-supreme-court/1721439.html); [LUC 2015 OSC page](https://luc.hawaii.gov/completed-dockets/boundary-amendments/oahu/a06-771/2015-osc/).
**Note: Earthjustice was NOT counsel of record in either LUC appeal** (contrary to a prior
guess) — representation was Dunne at the agency and the Seitz firm on appeal; the
Earthjustice tie is via Moriwake's volunteer legal-chair role and later Townsend's career.

**The chapter's own framing (verbatim).** Homepage, Wayback 2011-12-04 and 2012-03-11:
"**LET'S STOP URBAN SPRAWL!** It's time to say enough already. Let's preserve our farmland
for future generations... Some of the biggest developers in Hawaii are aggressively trying
to develop two new massive urban sprawl projects, which would eliminate some of the most
productive farmlands in the world. [Koa Ridge/Ho'opili]... Paves over prime agricultural
land... Enough is enough already. Let's protect our farms."
Civil Beat op-ed, 2011-07-27, "Hoopili and Koa Ridge Just Aren't Pono" (byline Robert
Harris; the chapter homepage credits it to "Anthony Aalto and Robert Harris"): "We opposed
Koa Ridge because it would have splurged 5,000 homes over some of our best remaining
farmland while making many of Oahu's most glaring problems even worse: traffic congestion,
pollution, over-dependence on ever-more expensive imported energy, **mounting food
insecurity**... Ho'opili... would plant 11,750 housing units on some of the most productive
farmland in the state – the place where an incredible **20% of our local produce** is
grown... Over the last 50 years... we paved 50 percent of our prime farmland."
([archived Civil Beat URL](http://www.civilbeat.com/posts/2011/07/27/12273-hoopili-koa-ridge-just-arent-pono/), via Wayback.)

Related conduct: chapter support for Honolulu rail was explicitly land-use-driven
(anti-sprawl, not transit merits) per Aalto's 2015 Star-Advertiser profile; and the 2015
Carleton Ching/DLNR fight put the Club directly against a Castle & Cooke lobbyist.

## 3. The SB 631 position in that context

Timeline lock-step: Koa Ridge LUC vote challenged Dec 2010 → Harris posts "A Food Marshall
Plan for Hawaii" on the chapter blog 2010-12-01 → SB 631 opposition testimony Feb–Mar 2011
→ Ho'opili intervention announced 2011 → "Stop Urban Sprawl / preserve our farmland"
becomes the chapter homepage through at least mid-2012.

The Capitol Watch blog's 2011 session wrap-up (posted 2011-05-02, Wayback capture) lists
SB 631 first under "**BAD BILLS THAT PASSED** CONFERENCE COMMITTEE":
> "Renewable Energy; Agriculture Land; Solar Energy Facilities (SB 631 SD1 HD1 CD1) Allows
> solar energy facilities to be built on ten percent of agricultural land classified as
> having 'B' or 'C' quality or up to twenty acres, which ever is lesser. **While we love
> solar energy facilities, we're concerned this could increase the cost of an already
> limited supply of prime farmland and squeeze out farmers that grow food. More analysis
> should have occurred before passing this bill.**"

Two analytical points:
1. The objection is a **land-price/rent argument** — identical in structure to the
   testimony language already verified ("new and more expensive 'highest and best uses'...
   established on agricultural land"). The Club's litigation posture in A06-771 and Koa
   Ridge turned on keeping ag-district land legally and economically dedicated to farming
   (HAR §15-15-77(b)(6)'s "substantially impair agricultural production" test; art. XI §3).
   Any statutory admission of higher-value non-farm uses into the ag district erodes
   exactly the premise their intervenor cases defended.
2. They condemned the bill **even with the 10%/20-acre cap** ("bad bill... more analysis
   should have occurred"). That weakly suggests the cap was not the Sierra Club's
   negotiated ask — they wanted the bill dead — though it does not identify who did ask
   for the cap (still the open Ground Zero question; committee reports needed).

Harris blog post, 2010-12-01 ("A Food Marshall Plan for Hawaii," posted by Scott Glenn,
Wayback): the State "is developing prime agricultural land... Economically, **food
production loses to both alternative agricultural uses, such as biofuels, and cheap
imports**... A plan with teeth and sweets will help Hawaii to reduce its vulnerability."
Notable: months before SB 631, the chapter was already classing *energy uses of ag land*
(biofuels) with development as competitors to food production.

## 4. Food-security frame genealogy (who supplied the vocabulary)

The Club adopted an ambient, state-sanctioned frame; it did not invent it:
- **CTAHR economics**: Leung & Loke, "Economic Impacts of Increasing Hawai'i's Food
  Self-Sufficiency," CTAHR Economic Issues EI-16, Dec 2008 ([PDF](https://www.ctahr.hawaii.edu/oc/freepubs/pdf/EI-16.pdf)) — source of the 85%-imported figure and the
  "replace 10% of imports = $188M sales / $47M earnings / ~2,300 jobs" arithmetic.
  **Lee & Bittenbender** (CTAHR): 2007 analysis of fresh fruit/vegetable imports vs local
  production; 2008 agriculture paper for the Hawai'i 2050 Sustainability Plan (cited in the
  2012 state strategy's Vol. II). These are the likely source of the "self-sufficiency
  acreage math" in the 2011 testimony — exact citation in the testimony: UNVERIFIED.
- **Statute**: Act 183 (2005) created the Important Agricultural Lands framework (art. XI
  §3 implementation); **Act 233 (2008)** added IAL incentives (refundable ag-cost tax
  credit certified up to $7.5M/yr, 85% loan guarantees, expedited permits, 15%
  reclassification ratchet) ([CTAHR summary](https://www.ctahr.hawaii.edu/AgLand/ial.html); [HDOA IAL page](https://dab.hawaii.gov/important-ag-lands-ial/)). The 2010 barrel-tax act
  creating an Agricultural Development & Food Security Special Fund fits here (the Club's
  2011 blog laments the death of a bill reallocating barrel-tax income) — act number
  UNVERIFIED in this note (check Act 73, SLH 2010).
- **Executive**: Office of Planning (DBEDT) + Dept. of Agriculture, "**Increased Food
  Security and Food Self-Sufficiency Strategy**," Oct 2012 ([PDF](https://files.hawaii.gov/dbedt/op/spb/INCREASED_FOOD_SECURITY_AND_FOOD_SELF_SUFFICIENCY_STRATEGY.pdf)) — 85–90%
  imported, "Buy Local, It Matters," increase-production objectives; prepared under HRS
  ch. 226 (Hawai'i State Plan). Abercrombie's 2010 "New Day" platform food-self-sufficiency
  plank: plausible, UNVERIFIED here. 2008 oil/food price spike as trigger: asserted in the
  2012 strategy's own framing.
- **Civil society**: Kanu Hawaii's annual "Eat Local Challenge"; Hawai'i Food Policy
  Council (founded ~2012, Ashley Lukens a founding member — founding year UNVERIFIED);
  Ulupono Initiative (Omidyar, est. 2009) with local-food production metrics; Hawai'i
  Farm Bureau. George Kent (UH) "Food Security in Hawai'i" as academic backdrop.
- **Economist pushback in-window: essentially null.** The serious methodological critique
  came from UH economists Loke & Leung themselves ("Hawai'i's food consumption and supply
  sources: benchmark estimates and measurement issues," *Agricultural and Food Economics*
  2013 — noting the ubiquitous 85–90% figures rest on questionable methodology), and later
  La Croix & Mak (UHERO, 2021). No contemporaneous (2008–2012) UHERO critique of the
  self-sufficiency frame located.

## 5. Positions on renewables over time (revealed pattern)

- **Pro-solar, non-ag venues**: chapter's 2011 about-us page claims credit for "a mandatory
  solar hot water heater on all new homes" (2008 mandate; act number UNVERIFIED). 2011
  session "good bills" per Capitol Watch: on-bill financing (HB 1520), PUC renewable
  cost-consideration (SB 1482); lamented the weakening of the PV-ready-homes bill (SB 181).
  Explicit: "we love solar energy facilities." Harris's bio credits the chapter with the
  aggressive RPS push; Harris exited to Sunrun (rooftop solar).
- **Rooftop solar/net metering**: chapter fought HECO/PUC over ending NEM; Townsend, Oct
  2015: "It is not clear why the PUC just threw net-metering off the cliff, when it has
  been so effective at moving people off fossil fuels" ([Renewable Energy World/RMI](https://www.renewableenergyworld.com/solar/hawaii-just-ended-net-metering-for-solar-now/)).
- **Wind**: no Sierra Club opposition found to Kahuku (2011) or Kawailoa (2012, on
  Kamehameha Schools land) wind farms; no chapter position located on "Big Wind"
  (Lāna'i/Moloka'i) or the interisland cable (absence = not located, not confirmed
  neutrality). **Na Pua Makani (Kahuku, 2019–21)**: the chapter was *not a party* to any
  appeal but stood "in solidarity with the many community groups... fighting for the safety
  of their communities," framing the issue as procedural/energy justice ("Hawai'i cannot
  achieve justice in our energy system until impacted communities are a true part of the
  decision making process"), not anti-wind ([chapter blog, Mar 2021](https://sierraclubhawaii.org/blog/npm-4121)).
- **2014 Act 52** (solar-on-ag liberalization w/ sunset): Sierra Club testimony **not
  located** (capitol.hawaii.gov blocked; no Wayback capture of 2014 Capitol Watch pages).
  OPEN ITEM — high value: did they oppose the SUP path on B/C soils as they opposed SB 631?
- **Agrivoltaics debates 2022–2025**: no chapter testimony located by web search; national
  Sierra Club published pro-agrivoltaics best-practice guidance (2023). OPEN ITEM.

Revealed pattern: **pro-renewables-everywhere-except-the-ag-district**, plus deference to
community opponents of specific projects. Consistent across 2008–2021.

## 6. Funding / structure null-check

Structure: the Hawai'i Chapter is a unit of the national Sierra Club (501(c)(4)); its
charitable funding flows through the national Sierra Club Foundation. The 2011 site shows a
small operation: P.O. Box, one-room office at 1040 Richards St., volunteer ExCom, newsletter
ads priced $50–$350, one paid director (Harris) plus a few staff.
**Null result (explicit)**: no evidence found of funding from Hawai'i landholders, estates,
trusts, or Hawaiian Electric to the chapter or its land-use program — no HCF or Harold K.L.
Castle Foundation grants to the chapter located by search. Revealed conduct points the
other way: the chapter litigated for years against Castle & Cooke (Koa Ridge) and D.R.
Horton, led the fight against a Castle & Cooke lobbyist's DLNR nomination (2015), and
fought HECO on net metering (2015). Caveats: chapter-level donor lists are not public;
Sierra Club Foundation grant detail by chapter was not examined (990 review would be the
next step); absence of evidence in web sources is weak evidence of absence.

## 7. Assessment: does the anti-sprawl-commitment-device hypothesis hold up?

**Largely yes, on revealed conduct.** The chapter's SB 631 opposition was authored by the
same two people (Harris, Aalto) who were simultaneously running the Koa Ridge/Ho'opili
campaign; the chapter's public identity in 2011–12 *was* "stop urban sprawl / preserve our
farmland"; and its stated objection to SB 631 — solar as a "new and more expensive
'highest and best use'" raising ag land prices — is precisely the economic premise its LUC
litigation needed to defend (ag district as a legally low-rent, farming-dedicated
category under HAR §15-15-77(b)(6) and art. XI §3). Their simultaneous pro-solar record
(rooftop, RPS, financing, "we love solar energy facilities") rules out anti-renewables
animus and marks the position as *venue-specific*: it is about what the ag district is
for, not about solar. The food-security vocabulary was off-the-shelf state discourse
(CTAHR 2007–08, Act 183/233, 2012 OP strategy) adopted at least by Dec 2010 (Harris's
"Food Marshall Plan" post — which tellingly already grouped *energy* uses, biofuels, with
imports as threats to food production).

Nuance: "commitment device" and "sincere food-systems belief" are observationally close
here, and the Club's leaders plainly also believed the food argument (Aalto still recited
the 50%-farmland-lost / 8–10%-local-food numbers in 2015, after both LUC cases were lost).
The defensible paper claim is the conjunction: *the chapter opposed solar on ag land with
the same frame, the same personnel, and in the same months as its litigation against
residential conversion of ag land; its opposition was to ag-district rent appreciation
from any higher-value use, of which solar was one.*

**What would falsify / sharpen it:**
1. Sierra Club testimony SUPPORTING solar on ag land in 2014 (Act 52) or in the 2022–25
   agrivoltaics bills *after* Koa Ridge/Ho'opili were finally lost (2015–16) → supports the
   commitment-device reading (position tracks litigation needs); continued opposition →
   supports sincere-food-policy reading. (Open item; pull 2014/2022+ testimony.)
2. Evidence the Club coordinated SB 631 positions with landholder or ag-lobby interests
   (LURF, Farm Bureau) — none found; the Aalto "duplicate" testimony is internal, and the
   Club's conduct toward the largest landholder-developers was adversarial.
3. Evidence the Club *asked for the 10%/20-acre cap* as a fallback in committee — its
   May 2011 "bad bills" write-up (condemning the capped bill) cuts against this, but only
   the AGL/ENE/WLH committee reports and full testimony can settle who requested the cap.
4. If the food-security frame in their testimony predates the sprawl fights (e.g., in
   Malama i ka Honua issues 2005–2008), the "frame adopted for the campaign" chronology
   weakens. (2011 newsletter PDFs were not archived by Wayback; chapter may have paper
   archives.)
