# Wind turbine setback rules in Hawaii (focus: Oahu post-Kahuku/Na Pua Makani)

Compiled 2026-07-11. Every claim sourced; UNVERIFIED tags where a primary source
could not be reached. Revealed conduct only (who sponsored, who testified);
no inferred intent.

Context anchor: Na Pua Makani (AES Corp., 8 turbines, 568-ft tip height, Kahuku,
operational 2020) triggered a monthslong road blockade in 2019. Arrest counts
reported: "more than 160 arrests" ([Civil Beat 2021-06](https://www.civilbeat.org/2021/06/this-north-shore-community-has-had-enough-of-towering-wind-turbines/));
"200 arrests" (attorney Ryan Hurley testimony on Bill 64, [MISC. COM. 298 P&E](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/document-download?id=21738));
"~200 arrests" in later Civil Beat reporting. AES states the closest turbine is
~1,700 ft from the closest home and ~1,750 ft from Kahuku High School
([AES/Na Pua Makani FAQ](https://www.napuamakanihawaii.org/faq/)).

## 1. Current rules — Oahu (ROH Ch. 21, as replaced by Ordinance 25-2)

**Ordinance 25-2** (= **Bill 64 (2023), CD2, FD2**, "Relating to Use Regulations" —
the wholesale LUO replacement) is the current law.

Legislative record (official ordinance PDF, verified 2026-07-17:
[Ordinance 25-002 / Bill 64 / LUO](https://www4.honolulu.gov/docushare/dsweb/Get/Document-354710/Ordinance%2025-002_Bill%2064_LUO.pdf),
City & County of Honolulu docushare; mirror: [ORD25-002.pdf](https://files.amlegal.com/pdffiles/Honolulu/ORD25-002.pdf);
hnldoc measure 2784 is now login-gated). Confirmed: Bill 64 (2023) is the
comprehensive revision of the Land Use Ordinance (ROH ch. 21) — the first since
1990 — not a targeted amendment:
- Introduced 11/28/2023 by **Esther Kiaʻāina** (chair, Planning & the Economy).
- Passed third reading 12/11/2024, **8 ayes – 1 no (Calvin Say)**. HPR reported
  Say's stated reason was disagreement with the added wind-turbine restrictions
  ([HPR 2024-12-12](https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2024-12-12/honolulu-city-council-overhauls-land-use-rules-for-construction-business-and-housing)).
- Transmitted to Mayor 12/20/2024; **returned approved 01/03/2025** (hnldoc measure page).

Wind provisions in the enrolled ordinance (text extracted from ORD25-002.pdf,
"Wind energy generation" standards; three tiers by rated capacity):

| Tier | Capacity | Setback |
|---|---|---|
| Small | ≤ 15 kW | ≥ 1× facility height from all property lines |
| Medium | 15–99 kW (ag, country, industrial, industrial-MU districts only) | ≥ 1× height from all property lines |
| Large | ≥ 100 kW | (i) ≥ 1× height from **all** property lines, AND (ii) **≥ 10× height or 1.25 miles, whichever is GREATER**, from the property lines of any zoning lot in the **country, residential, apartment, apartment mixed-use, or resort** districts |

- Height = tower plus farthest vertical extension of blade tip (i.e., tip height).
- Large facilities are **not permitted** in preservation, residential, apartment,
  apartment-MU, business, business-MU, resort, industrial, or industrial-MU districts
  (i.e., effectively agricultural and country land only, subject to the setbacks).
- Operating limits at pre-existing occupied buildings: shadow flicker ≤ 10 hr/yr
  or 10 min/day; noise ≤ 10 dBA above ambient; conformity must be demonstrated with
  site-specific modeling (WindPro/iNoise) before a conditional use permit issues.
- **Repowering clamp:** legally established facilities may increase height during
  the term of the facility's *current* PPA only, by ≤ 7% of original height or to
  575 ft max, whichever is less. (Kahuku's tallest turbines are 568 ft.)
- **Waiver ban:** wind energy generation facilities are expressly *ineligible* for
  the director's waiver of development/design standards (ORD25-002, amendment to
  the LUO waiver section). This closes the mechanism by which Na Pua Makani
  obtained reduced setbacks (see §5 below).
- AG-1/AG-2 lots hosting wind must keep a minimum of 50 percent [OCR reads
  "51"/"5i" — **UNVERIFIED which of 50/51**] of crop-suitable lot area in active
  agricultural use, evidenced by a farm plan.

**Prior rule (pre-2025):** "wind machines" required a setback from all property
lines equal to the height of the machine (1:1 tip height), with >100 kW machines
requiring a major conditional use permit. 1:1 baseline confirmed by the enrolled
ordinance's digest of prior law and press reporting
([Civil Beat 2022-09](https://www.civilbeat.org/2022/09/the-struggle-over-towering-wind-farms-is-at-the-center-of-a-honolulu-city-council-debate/),
[wind-watch/Star-Adv 2024-12-18](https://www.wind-watch.org/news/2024/12/18/honolulu-city-council-increases-setback-requirements-kahuku-wind-turbines-will-be-removed-at-end-of-power-contracts-in-2031-and-2040/)).
Exact former section number (believed §21-5.720-series) — **UNVERIFIED**.

Consequence reported by Civil Beat: under the 1.25-mile standard **18 of Kahuku's
20 turbines become nonconforming** ([Civil Beat 2024-06](https://www.civilbeat.org/2024/06/tweaks-to-honolulus-land-use-law-could-jeopardize-kahukus-controversial-wind-farms/));
because nonconforming turbines cannot repower past their current PPAs, Oahu's
existing wind fleet is expected to come down as PPAs expire — first batch 2031,
last ~2040; the ~69 MW Kawailoa/Pupukea-area facility is also affected (three-farm fleet ≈127 MW, not 155)
([Civil Beat 2024-12](https://civilbeat.org/2024/12/wind-turbines-down-threatening-renewable-energy-goals/)).

## 2. Proposed county measures (Honolulu), 2019–2024

Chronological chain (all primary-source-verified via hnldoc unless noted):

1. **Resolution 19-305** — introduced by **Heidi Tsuneyoshi** (Kahuku's council
   member); proposes LUO amendment requiring all future wind machines to be
   located **5 miles from any property boundary**. Adopted **5/20/2020, 9 ayes**
   ([docushare record](https://www4.honolulu.gov/docushare/dsweb/Get/Document-245549/RES19-305.htm);
   setback content per DPP status memo
   [DEPT. COM. 128 (2023)](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/document-download?id=16649)).
   City officials warned a 5-mile rule "would essentially eliminate future
   development of wind farms anywhere on Oahu"
   ([Star-Advertiser 2020-03-08](https://www.staradvertiser.com/2020/03/08/hawaii-news/zoning-committee-decision-could-restrict-wind-farms-on-oahu/)).
   Planning Commission forwarded its recommendation to Council 5/20/2021 (DPP memo).

2. **Bill 28 (2021)** — intro 5/26/2021 by **Tommy Waters "By Request"** (from the
   Planning Commission/DPP process): setback of **1.25 miles** from residential
   properties. Passed first reading 6/2/2021 (9-0); no further action; **filed
   (died) 5/26/2023** under the 2-year rule
   ([hnldoc measure 1871](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/measure/1871)).

3. **Bill 29 (2021)** — intro 5/26/2021 by **Waters "By Request"**: **1,500 ft or
   2× system height, whichever greater, from all property lines**. First reading
   6/2/2021; **filed 5/26/2023**
   ([hnldoc measure 1872](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/measure/1872)).

4. **Bill 30 (2021)** — intro 5/26/2021 by **Tsuneyoshi**: **5 miles from all
   property lines**. First reading 6/2/2021; **filed 5/26/2023**
   ([hnldoc measure 1873](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/measure/1873)).
   Setback attributions for Bills 28/29/30 per DPP status memo (id=16649) and
   [Civil Beat 2021-06](https://www.civilbeat.org/2021/06/this-north-shore-community-has-had-enough-of-towering-wind-turbines/).

5. **Bill 10 (2022)** — intro 2/3/2022 by **Waters "By Request"** (DPP's first
   full-LUO rewrite attempt). CD1 kept 1× height for small/medium wind and set
   large-wind setback at 1× height from all lines **plus 1 mile** (instead of
   1.25 mi) from country/residential/apartment/apartment-MU/resort lots
   ([Bill 10 CD1 committee summary](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/document-download?id=14666)).
   Amended to CD2 and postponed 9/26/2022 and 10/20/2022; died with the council
   term ([hnldoc measure 2083](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/measure/2083);
   [Civil Beat 2022-09-28](https://www.civilbeat.org/2022/09/honolulu-city-council-puts-controversial-land-use-bill-on-hold-amid-criticism/)).
   In a September 2022 straw poll during Bill 10 deliberations, 7 members favored
   1.25 miles; **Brandon Elefante and Calvin Say** favored 1 mile
   ([Civil Beat 2022-09](https://www.civilbeat.org/2022/09/the-struggle-over-towering-wind-farms-is-at-the-center-of-a-honolulu-city-council-debate/)).

6. **Bill 55 (2022)** — "Relating to Wind Machines," intro 9/30/2022 by
   **Andria Tupola**: standalone **1.25-mile** setback. Passed first reading
   11/2/2022 (9-0), reported out of Zoning 11/17/2022; no final action; **filed
   9/30/2024** ([hnldoc measure 2338](https://hnldoc.ehawaii.gov/hnldoc/measure/2338)).

7. **Bill 64 (2023)** → **Ordinance 25-2** (see §1). The enacted large-wind
   setback (max of 10× height, 1.25 mi) is stricter than every prior proposal
   except the 5-mile bills.

## 3. State legislature bills (capitol.hawaii.gov), 2020–2026

- **HB 2188 (2020)** — "Relating to Wind Energy Facilities." Introduced by
  **Quinlan** (D-North Shore) + Brower, Eli, Gates, Hashimoto, Holt, Ichiyama,
  Johanson, Kitagawa, C. Lee, Lowen, Matayoshi, Mizuno, Morikawa, Nakashima,
  Takayama, San Buenaventura. Amends **HRS § 205-4.5(a)** (ag district permitted
  uses). As introduced: wind turbines ≥ 1 MW must be **≥ 1 mile from the nearest
  farm dwelling or off-site residential dwelling** existing at application,
  center-of-turbine to dwelling exterior
  ([bill text](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2020/bills/HB2188_.HTM)).
  Also mandated a JABSOM turbine-noise health study. **HD1 left the distance
  blank** ([HD1 text](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2020/bills/HB2188_HD1_.HTM));
  passed House third reading 2/27/2020; **Senate EET amended (SD1) to 1 ft per
  1 ft of turbine height** — i.e., back to the counties' existing 1:1 standard
  ([SD1 text](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2020/bills/HB2188_SD1_.HTM));
  **died in Senate WAM** (last action 6/22/2020, COVID-truncated session;
  [status page](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session/measure_indiv.aspx?billtype=HB&billnumber=2188&year=2020)).
  Defective effective date 7/1/2050 in HD1/SD1.

- **SB 2805 (2020)** — Riviere, Harimoto, Ihara, Kim, Moriwaki, Ruderman,
  L. Thielen. Not a setback bill: required Office of Planning to prepare a
  comprehensive renewable-energy siting plan (findings cited Kahuku/Na Pua Makani
  and turbines "as close as 1,645 feet" from residences, per
  [LegiScan text](https://legiscan.com/HI/text/SB2805/id/2131745)). SD1 passed
  two committees; **deferred (died) in CPH 2/20/2020**
  ([status page](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session/measure_indiv.aspx?billtype=SB&billnumber=2805&year=2020)).

- **SB 957 (2021–2022)** — Introduced by **Riviere** (North Shore senator) with
  Acasio, Fevella, Lee, Ihara, Kim, Moriwaki, Shimabukuro, Taniguchi. Would add
  a section to **HRS ch. 46** requiring **each county** to adopt ordinances
  setting wind turbines back **≥ 10 ft per 1 ft of system height (10× tip
  height) from all property lines on urban- or rural-classified land**
  ([bill text](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2022/bills/SB957_.HTM)).
  Referred PSM/EET, JDC; carried over to 2022; **never heard — died**
  ([status page](https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session/measure_indiv.aspx?billtype=SB&billnumber=957&year=2022)).
  Note the 10:1 ratio later enacted (for Oahu, alongside the 1.25-mi floor and
  limited to sensitive-district lot lines) in Ordinance 25-2.

- **2023–2026 sessions:** no state wind-setback or wind-moratorium bill located
  in searches (terms: wind setback, wind energy facility, 205-4.5 amendments).
  **Not exhaustively verified** — capitol.hawaii.gov blocks automated fetching;
  a manual keyword sweep of 2023–2026 measure lists is a remaining task.

## 6. Severity quantification

Reference turbine: modern onshore machine, tip height 150–200 m (492–656 ft).
Kahuku's Na Pua Makani units: 568 ft (173 m).

| Rule | Formula | Implied distance (200 m tip) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old Oahu LUO (pre-2025) | 1× tip height, all lines | 200 m / 656 ft | Waivable by DPP (Na Pua Makani: waived to 275 ft per KNSC) |
| HB 2188 (2020, intro) | 1 mile from dwellings | 1,609 m | Dwellings only, ag district, ≥1 MW |
| HB 2188 SD1 (2020) | 1× height from dwellings | 200 m | Reversion to status quo ratio |
| Bill 29 (2021) | max(1,500 ft, 2× height) | 400 m | All property lines |
| Bill 10 (2022) CD1 | 1× all lines + 1 mi from sensitive districts | 1,609 m | |
| Bills 28 (2021), 55 (2022) | 1.25 mi from residential | 2,012 m | |
| SB 957 (2021-22, state) | 10× height, all lines (urban/rural land) | 2,000 m | Died |
| **Ord 25-2 (enacted)** | max(10× height, 1.25 mi) from country/res/apt/resort lots; 1× all lines | **2,012 m** | 1.25-mi floor binds for tip heights < 660 ft; 10× binds above |
| Res 19-305 / Bill 30 | 5 mi from any property line | 8,047 m | Officials: would end wind development on Oahu (Star-Adv 2020-03-08) |

Geometry: the enacted rule excludes a ~2 km (1.25 mi) buffer — ~12.7 km² — around
*every* country/residential/apartment/resort-zoned lot line. For comparison, Oahu
is ~1,545 km². Enacted-rule bite, per reporting: **18 of 20 existing Kahuku
turbines nonconforming** (Civil Beat 2024-06); all three Oahu wind farms
(Kahuku Wind ~30 MW/12 turbines, Na Pua Makani ~27.6 MW/8 turbines (8×3.45),
Kawailoa ~69 MW/30 turbines; fleet total ≈127 MW) expected off by ~2031–2040 with repowering foreclosed
(Civil Beat 2024-12). **No published GIS analysis quantifying remaining
wind-developable Oahu land under the 1.25-mi rule was located** (HSEO/DPP made
qualitative statements only; Civil Beat 2022-09 noted future proposals would
likely shift to the island's west side). Computing this from parcel + zoning
shapefiles is feasible follow-on work (Honolulu open GIS data).

Relevance to the project's wind/solar asymmetry argument: HRS § 205-4.5(a)(15)
still permits wind on ag land with no state acreage cap or soil-class ban, but
since January 2025 the *county* LUO on Oahu imposes on wind a siting constraint
far more severe than the state's 10%/20-acre solar cap in most populated
areas — the asymmetry has effectively reversed at the county level, driven by
documented organic community mobilization (contrast with the solar record).

## 7. GIS quantification of the Ord 25-2 screen (this project, 2026-07)

Computed in `analysis/wind_viable_map.py` (map: `analysis/figs/paper/f_wind_map.png`;
tables: `data/gis/wind_viable_areas.csv`, `data/gis/wind_setback_oahu.csv`).
Applying the enacted rule — AG-1/AG-2/Country districts only, ≥ 1.25 mi from any
country/residential/apartment/apartment-MU/resort zoning-lot line (flat floor;
10× tip ≈ 2,000 m for ~200 m tips) — **39,386 ac remain geometrically viable,
36.3% of Oahu's 108,417 ac of AG/Country-zoned land**, vs ~91,856 ac (84.7%)
under the pre-2025 ~1×-tip-height (~200 m) rule approximated against the same
sensitive set. Because Country is itself a protected district, every C-zoned acre
is within 1.25 mi of a C lot line: all viable land is AG-1/AG-2. Overlaying the
10 m USGS slope raster, **26,023 ac (66%) of the viable land is ≤ 30% slope**;
13,363 ac is steeper (buildable, if at all, only along ridge access). The viable
land is entirely the mauka interior of the island's north-central quadrant, in
four main blocks: (1) the **Waialua/Helemano plateau** (~21,200 ac, the largest by
far, but 39% of it >30% slope in the Koʻolau-foothill gulches; served only by
mapped 46 kV); (2) **Kunia/central Oahu** (~7,800 ac, 89% ≤30% slope — the
gentlest large block, and the closest to the 138 kV spine); (3) the **Kahuku
hills** (~4,100 ac, but majority-steep at 53% >30%) mauka of the existing
turbines; and (4) **Kawailoa/Pupukea mauka** (~4,000 ac). Small remnants sit in
the Waiʻanae Range above Mākua (~1,000 ac), Waipiʻo/Waiawa (~870 ac, mostly
steep), and Nānākuli (~200 ac). The three existing farms (Kahuku Wind,
Na Pua Makani, Kawailoa) sit at or just outside the edges of the Kahuku and
Kawailoa viable blocks — the Kahuku-area markers fall inside the 1.25-mi
exclusion, consistent with Civil Beat's report that 18 of 20 Kahuku turbines are
nonconforming; the Kawailoa marker falls near the boundary of the screened-viable
area, but the repowering clamp and waiver ban apply regardless. Caveats: geometric
screen only (no FAA/radar, noise-at-receptor, habitat, or cultural layers);
zoning-lot boundaries proxy for property lines; C&C Honolulu open-data LUO layer
(2024/25 vintage); the pre-2025 figure is an upper-bound-flavored approximation
(see `analysis/wind_setback_oahu.py` docstring).
