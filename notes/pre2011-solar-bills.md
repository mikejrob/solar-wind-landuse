# Pre-2011 attempts to open Hawaii ag land to solar/renewables (and early post-2011 follow-ons)

Compiled 2026-07-11. Thread: failed or altered liberalization attempts before SB 631 (2011,
Act 217), sessions ~2003–2010, plus 2012–2013 follow-on bills.

## Method / source note (read first)

- capitol.hawaii.gov (including data.capitol.hawaii.gov) returns Cloudflare 403 to all
  non-browser clients as of 2026-07-11. **Every capitol document below was retrieved from the
  Internet Archive Wayback Machine**, citing the original capitol URL + Wayback capture
  timestamp. Raw files cached under `data/raw/capitol/` (subdirs per bill).
- Wayback fetch pattern: `https://web.archive.org/web/<TIMESTAMP>id_/<ORIGINAL_URL>`.
- Discovery of bill numbers was via (a) diffing Wayback snapshots of HRS §§ 205-2 / 205-4.5
  across 2003–2015, (b) the archived official 2008 acts-by-bill list, (c) web search of
  Google-indexed capitol/LegiScan pages. **Coverage caveat:** no complete keyword sweep of all
  bill titles for 2003–2010 was possible (the archived per-session keyword-search pages are
  not replayable). Negative findings below ("none found") are search-limited, not proven
  exhaustive. A definitive sweep needs either unblocked capitol access or the LRB Digest and
  Index volumes for each session.

## 0. Statutory baseline from dated Wayback snapshots of HRS § 205-4.5

Snapshot files: `data/raw/capitol/hrs_205-4.5_wayback_<ts>.htm`
(ts = 20030130231954, 20060408224341, 20070208224043, 20080723202929, 20091026094232,
20100125195459, 20150601021944; original URL
http://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/hrscurrent/Vol04_Ch0201-0257/HRS0205/HRS_0205-0004_0005.htm)

- **Jan 2003** (history line ends `am L 1997, c 258, §11`): wind energy facilities already a
  permitted use — item (a)(12), **no proviso, no acreage cap, no soil-class limit**. Solar:
  absent entirely; § 205-4.5(b) ("uses not expressly permitted... shall be prohibited except
  205-6 / 205-8") = solar excluded from A/B soils. Wind therefore entered § 205-4.5 via one of
  L 1980 c 24, L 1982 c 217, L 1991 c 281, or L 1997 c 258 — **which act: still open**
  (UNVERIFIED; check SLH volumes).
- **Apr 2006** (+ L 2005 c 205): still no wind proviso, no solar.
- **Feb 2007** (+ L 2006 c 237 §4, c 250 §2, c 271 §1): wind item now carries the proviso
  "compatible with agriculture uses and cause minimal adverse impact on agricultural land."
  → **The wind proviso was added by one of the three 2006 acts (c 237, c 250, c 271); which
  one is UNVERIFIED** (good lead for the wind-asymmetry thread — it was 2006, not 2005).
- **Jul 2008** (+ L 2007 c 159 §3, c 171 §1): new item (15) biofuel processing facilities.
- **Oct 2009** (+ L 2008 c 145 §3): new item (16) agricultural-energy facilities with the
  **90%-of-acreage agricultural-primacy rule** (see SB 2849 below); plantation community
  subdivision rewrite.
- **Jan 2010** (+ L 2009 c 53 §1): only style edits visible in diff ("including but not
  limited to" → "including") — c 53 = the annual Revision Bill (HB 1199 2009, see below).
- Solar first appears in § 205-4.5 in the post-2011 snapshot (L 2011 c 217 = SB 631).

Parallel: § 205-2(d) already contained "wind generated energy production for public, private,
and commercial use" before 2005 (shown as pre-existing text in Act 205 (2005), SLH PDF below).
Solar entered § 205-2(d)(6) in 2008 via Act 31 (HB 2502), D/E soils only (coordinator's
snapshot diff of § 205-2 confirms: no solar in Sept 2007 snapshot, present by Oct 2009).

## 3. 2008 — HB 2502 → Act 31 (SLH 2008): solar opened on D/E soils only — THE KEY PRE-2011 EPISODE

Cached under `data/raw/capitol/2008_HB2502/`. Status page (introducers, full timeline):
http://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2008/lists/getstatus2.asp?billno=HB2502
(Wayback 20080916133308).

- **As introduced** (HB2502_.htm, Wayback 20081026215109): amends § 205-2(d) to add
  "(6) Solar energy facilities; provided that this paragraph shall apply only to land with
  soil classified... class D or E." **The D/E-only limit was in the bill from day one** — a
  Joint House-Senate Majority Caucus package bill. 41 House introducers, MORITA first
  (Hermina Morita, chair, House Energy & Environmental Protection). Companion: SB 2847.
- **HD1 (House EEP, Morita's own committee), HSCR 452-08** (Wayback 20081018001448):
  committee **INSERTED restrictions**: (1) cap = lesser of **10 acres or 1% of parcel**;
  (2) must be near existing transmission/distribution; (3) minimum insolation thresholds.
  Reasoning quoted: "lands classified... D or E are not necessarily unfit for agricultural
  use. To protect productive agricultural land, your Committee has amended..." Testifiers at
  EEP 2/5/08 per report: SUPPORT — Hawaii Solar Energy Assn, Hawaii Renewable Energy
  Alliance, **Hawaiian Electric Co. + Maui Electric + Hawaii Electric Light** (HECO testified
  it "supports H.B. 2502 and other similar bills"), Sopogy Inc., Castle & Cooke Hawaii,
  Dowling Company. COMMENTS — Dept of Agriculture.
- **DOA endorsed the cap** (testimony PDF, WLH-AGR 2/20/08, Wayback 20220303181040): Chair
  Sandra Lee Kunimoto: the 10-acre/1% provision "makes it less likely that... a solar energy
  facility will become the primary use on productive agricultural lands that happen to have
  'D' or 'E' ratings," and proposed further conditions (compatibility etc.). → **DOA is the
  earliest documented advocate of acreage-cap logic for solar on ag land (2008), three years
  before the SB 631 cap.**
- **Hawaii Farm Bureau Federation (Alan Takemoto): "does not support HB 2502, HD 1"** even
  with the cap — wants solar to stay in "the existing land use process" (i.e., special
  permits). **Maui County Farm Bureau (Warren Watanabe): strongly OPPOSED**, both at EEP
  (late file) and WLH/AGR. Same PDFs.
- **HD2 (House WLH + AGR, chairs Tsuji & Ito), HSCR 761-08** (Wayback 20081018084833):
  committees **REMOVED the acreage, location, and generation limits** — restoring the clean
  D/E-only permitted use. On record there: SUPPORT — Chamber of Commerce, Castle & Cooke,
  Sopogy; intent — DOA, Dowling; **OPPOSE — Maui County Farm Bureau**; comments — HFBF.
- **Senate AGR-HI/ENE (chairs Tokuda & Menor), SSCR 3396** (Wayback 20081017044047): passed
  HD2 **unamended**. Support included **Land Use Research Foundation of Hawaii (LURF)**,
  Dowling, Castle & Cooke, Chamber, Sopogy, UH Hawaii Energy Policy Forum, SunPower, Pacific
  Resource Partnership, HREA; HFBF intent; DOA support w/ amendment (not taken).
- Became **Act 31 (2008)** per archived official acts-by-bill list
  (http://capitol.hawaii.gov/session2008/lists/acts_list_bybill.asp, Wayback 20091031041503;
  cached `acts_list_bybill_2008.html`): "031 — HB2502 HD2 — RELATING TO SOLAR ENERGY."
- Interpretation for the paper (conduct only): in 2008 a cap was proposed (EEP + DOA) and
  **rejected** by the ag-committee chairs; the utility supported liberalization; LURF
  supported liberalization (of marginal lands); farm bureaus opposed. The binding constraint
  kept in place was the **soil-class line** (D/E only) — present from introduction — which
  left A/B/C soils closed to solar until 2011 (B/C via cap/SUP) and A closed to date.

## 4. 2008 — SB 2849 → Act 145 (SLH 2008): the "agricultural-energy facility" template (renewables subordinated to agriculture)

Cached under `data/raw/capitol/2008_SB2849/`. Status: getstatus2.asp?billno=SB2849
(Wayback 20080916131437). Introducers: MENOR, Baker, Chun Oakland, English, Espero, Fukunaga,
Gabbard, Hanabusa, Hooser, Ige, Ihara, Inouye, Kim, Kokubun, Nishihara, Sakamoto, Tokuda,
Tsutsui, Bunda, Hee, Taniguchi (Senate Majority package).

- **As introduced** (Wayback 20081022022340): already contains the full design later
  criticized as restrictive — ag-energy facilities permitted in ag districts only if the
  "primary activity" is agricultural, defined as **≥90% of enterprise acreage devoted to
  agricultural activity**, facility limited to lands of the entity conducting the ag
  activity, energy must come "from products of agricultural activities." **The 90% rule was the bill's premise. It was not a committee
  insertion.**
- SSCR 2700 (Wayback 20201029190335): SUPPORT — **Hawaii Farm Bureau Federation, Maui County
  Farm Bureau, Hawaiian Electric Co.**; intent — DOA; HARC sought Del Monte
  plantation-village amendment (taken; added plantation-subdivision provisions).
- HSCR 1309-08 (Wayback 20201029174715): SUPPORT — HFBF, HECO, MECO, HELCO, HARC, Pacific
  West Energy LLC; intent — DOA; comments — Office of Planning, Honolulu DPP, Hawaii's
  Thousand Friends.
- Became **Act 145 (2008)** (acts-by-bill list, same source as above): amended § 205-2 §2 and
  § 205-4.5 §3 (HRS history line `am L 2008, c 145, §3`).
- Pattern: the farm lobby and the utility jointly supported the *accessory* model
  (energy allowed only as adjunct to farming). Contrast with their split on HB 2502's
  *stand-alone* solar model. SB 631 (2011) as introduced ("whether or not agricultural
  activity is the primary activity") reads as a direct repudiation of Act 145's design.

## 8. 2011 anchor (cross-reference to coordinator's thread)

SB 631 → Act 217 (2011): introduced with renewable energy production as permitted use, no
cap; 10%/20-acre cap inserted in committee (see main SB 631 workstream). The 2008 record
above supplies the prehistory: cap logic first articulated by House EEP + DOA in 2008 and
rejected; farm-bureau opposition to stand-alone solar on any ag soil documented 2008.

## 10. Pattern summary

1. Solar-on-ag-land liberalization begins in 2008 (HCEI year); nothing earlier was found. Before that,
   wind was permitted uncapped (pre-1997 entry; proviso added 2006), biofuels 2007.
2. Both 2008 openings were **born narrow**: HB 2502 was D/E-only from introduction;
   SB 2849's 90% ag-primacy rule was in the introduced draft. The drafting compromise
   happened *before* introduction (majority-caucus packages), limiting what committee
   records can reveal about who set the soil-class line.
3. The acreage-cap instrument has a 2008 paper trail: proposed by House EEP (HSCR 452-08),
   endorsed by DOA in writing, **removed** by the House ag/water-land committees. In 2011
   the same instrument was inserted and survived. Comparing the 2008 vs 2011 testimony
   rosters on the cap is the sharpest identification opportunity in this prehistory.
4. Revealed positions, 2008: HECO/MECO/HELCO supported both solar bills (contra a naive
   rate-base story at this stage); LURF supported D/E liberalization; farm bureaus opposed
   stand-alone solar even on the worst soils but supported the accessory (90%) model;
   DOA consistently favored conditions/caps; large landowner-developers (Castle & Cooke,
   Dowling) and solar industry pushed to liberalize.
5. 2009–2010: apparent legislative pause on solar-ag siting (budget-crisis sessions);
   no found attempt to extend solar beyond D/E until SB 631 (2011).
