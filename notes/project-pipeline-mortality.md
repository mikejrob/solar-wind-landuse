# Where Oahu Utility-Scale Solar Actually Dies: A Pipeline-Stage Mortality Analysis

Compiled 2026-07-18. Companion data: `data/oahu_solar_project_pipeline.csv`.
Builds on `notes/sup-census.md` (LUC = rubber stamp: 7 approved / 1 pending / 0 denied /
0 intervenors), `notes/legal-representation-map.md` (opposition bar is Maui-only),
and `notes/oahu-grid-public-record.md` (transmission constraints).

**Evidentiary discipline.** Every factual claim carries a source URL. I distinguish
three failure modes throughout:
- **CANCELLED** — the PPA/contract was terminated by HECO or the developer (an economic
  or business decision).
- **BLOCKED** — a project stopped by opposition, permit denial, or a court vacating a
  permit (a veto by a third party or regulator).
- **DELAYED** — behind schedule but still alive.

## The framing correction this note delivers

The state Land Use Commission special-permit tier — the venue the earlier census
examined exhaustively — is a **near-zero-mortality stage**: it approves every solar SUP,
unanimously, in ≤45 days, with no intervenors ever. If you only looked at the LUC you
would conclude Hawaii solar sails through. It does not. **The mortality is almost
entirely UPSTREAM of the LUC, at HECO's competitive procurement, and the delay that
occasionally becomes fatal is at the COUNTY permit / community-opposition tier — but the
latter is a NEIGHBOR-ISLAND (Maui) phenomenon, nearly absent on Oahu.** On Oahu,
solar dies of economics inside HECO's contracting funnel. Opposition does not kill it.

## Stage 1 — HECO competitive procurement (THE dominant mortality stage)

All three RFP "stages" run under a **single PUC docket, No. 2017-0352** (the "2018-0434"
in earlier notes is a project-specific PPA sub-docket, Mililani I). Source:
https://puc.hawaii.gov/energy/renewable-energy-procurement/

This is the gate before any land-use permit exists, and it is where the overwhelming
majority of Oahu solar projects that "die" actually die — as **cancellations**, not
blocks.

### The 2016 precursor: HECO terminates 3 Oahu solar farms

Before the formal RFP stages, the earliest utility-scale Oahu solar cohort (First
Wind → SunEdison FIT/RFP projects) shows the pattern in its purest form. On
**2016-02-12 HECO issued termination notices to three Oahu solar farms at once —
Waiawa, Mililani, and Kawailoa, 112 MW combined** — for missing a construction-financing
milestone, even as buyer D.E. Shaw offered a 9% price cut and firm completion dates. **PUC
staff excoriated HECO**, calling the terminations "premature," "summarily issued," and not
"serving the best interests of the state," noting HECO showed no "sense of urgency."
SunEdison had already sunk >$42M in site work + $31.4M in interconnection payments to
HECO. All three were later revived and built by Clearway (2019–2022).
Source: https://www.environment-hawaii.org/?p=9006
→ Revealed conduct: the utility, as sole offtaker, holds a unilateral termination lever
and used it against fully-financed Oahu solar. This is the rate-base-relevant chokepoint.

### Stage 1 RFP (selected 2018): 0 of 4 Oahu solar projects lost

The four Oahu Stage-1 solar+storage awards — AES West Oahu (12.5 MW), Mililani I /
Clearway (39 MW), Waiawa / Clearway (36 MW), Hoʻohana / 174 Power Global (52 MW) — **all
reached commercial operation** (Hoʻohana last, COD 2025-07-11), though several slipped
years on supply-chain delays. Stage 1 attrition on Oahu: **zero**.
Source: https://www.hawaiianelectric.com/new-solar-battery-storage-project-completes-first-stage-of-renewable-energy-projects-on-3-island-grids

### Stage 3 RFP (Final Award Group Dec 2023): 7 Oahu projects; ≥1 already cancelled

Stage 3 selected 7 Oahu projects (of 15 statewide, >1,170 MW / 2,144 MWh). Already lost:
**Makana Lā Solar (Clearway, 80 MW / 480 MWh, Oahu), negotiations ended 2024-10-29**, one
of three Clearway projects (195 MW total) dropped citing **HECO's financial distress and
below-investment-grade credit after the August 2023 Maui wildfires** — a new,
utility-solvency cause distinct from supply chain. HECO chief energy officer Mark Glick

### Ranked causes of procurement-stage mortality (all islands)

1. **Supply-chain disruption + equipment/inflation cost spikes (2021–22)** — the single
   largest driver (Mahi, Barbers Point, Pulehu, Kahana, contributing to Puakō). Panel
   prices +57% (2021), shipping ~+500%, plus the U.S. anti-circumvention tariff probe.
2. **Utility financial/credit risk after the 2023 Maui wildfires** — HECO below
   investment grade; developers doubted HECO could finance/offtake (Clearway trio incl.
   Oahu's Makana Lā, Oct 2024).
3. **Contract/pricing disputes** — developers seeking above-approved-PPA prices, then
   walking (Longroad/Mahi). Overlaps with #1.
4. **Interconnection cost/delay** — cited explicitly for Puakō (Hawaii Island); see Stage 4.
5. **On-site cultural resources** — Kupehau (Oahu, the one clean Oahu block).

## Stage 2 — PUC PPA approval (a low-mortality filter, but the intervention right is a lever)

Solar PPAs go to the PUC for review; most are approved by Decision & Order without a
contested case. **The PUC almost never denies a solar PPA.** But a third party can seek to
**intervene** and force a contested case, which adds months and creates either a settlement
chokepoint or an appellate hook. In the entire Oahu/Maui Stage-era solar cohort, only
**two** PUC PPA dockets drew community intervention — **both on Maui** (Paeahu, Kahana),
neither of which was actually *denied* by the PUC.

### Paeahu Solar (Maui) — the canonical case (detail)

15 MW solar + 60 MWh storage on Ulupalakua Ranch land above the Maui Meadows subdivision,
South Maui; developer Innergex; offtaker MECO; a 2018 Stage-1 award. **PUC docket
2018-0433.**

- **PUC track:** Approved by D&O 37340 (Oct 2020); reconsideration denied (Order 37553).
  **Pono Power Coalition** (Kīhei community group, 700+ members) had *participant* status
  (the PUC recognized its members' clean-and-healthful-environment right but denied full
  intervenor status). On appeal the **Hawaii Supreme Court AFFIRMED the PUC**,
  **SCOT-21-0000041, decided 2022-03-02, Eddins J.; Wilson J. dissenting.** The Court (i)
  **declined to import Sherman Act "rule of reason" antitrust review** into PPA approval —
  rejecting Pono Power's argument that Stage-1 winners' post-selection use of **common
  counsel** to negotiate non-price terms was anticompetitive; and (ii) held the PUC
  discharged its public-trust duty via the HRS §§ 269-6(b)/269-145.5(b) statutory
  balancing where harms were "speculative." Wilson J. would have required the PUC to
  independently investigate wildfire/flood trust-resource risks (precautionary principle).
  Sources: https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/hi-supreme-court/2164364.html ;
  https://law.justia.com/cases/hawaii/supreme-court/2022/scot-21-0000041.html
  - Counsel (appeal): **Lance D. Collins + Bianca K. Isaki** for Pono Power; Kobayashi
    Sugita & Goda for MECO; Schlack Ito for Paeahu Solar.
- **COUNTY track (where opposition actually bit):** Pono Power + **Maui Meadows
  Neighborhood Association** challenged the Maui Planning Commission's special-use and
  Phase-II permits after the Commission **denied them intervention**. **2nd Circuit Judge
  Kelsey Kawano VACATED the county permits (~late Jan 2022)** and remanded with orders to
  grant intervention → hearing officer **Glenn Kosaka**, mediator John Rapacz; mediation
  failed; contested-case hearing began Nov 2022. County-track counsel were all
  **intervenor-side**: **Christina Lizzi (+ Linda Nye)** for Pono Power/Maui Meadows,
  **Ryan Hurley** for additional intervenors Maui Tomorrow Foundation + Hoʻoponopono O
  Mākena. (Correction to prior notes: Lizzi/Hurley were NOT applicant counsel.)
  Arguments: wildfire risk (explicit California-utility-fire analogy, pre-Lahaina),
  flooding of the downslope subdivision, cultural, endangered species.
  Sources: https://mauinow.com/2022/01/28/court-decision-delays-paeahu-solar-project/ ;
  https://www.civilbeat.org/2023/03/maui-solar-project-stokes-concerns-about-climate-fueled-fire-and-flooding-risks/
- **OUTCOME — CANCELLED 2023-11-28:** HECO and Innergex jointly ended the contract,
  citing "lengthy delays resulting from developer legal challenges along with
  pandemic-related cost and supply-chain issues." Innergex did not commit to reviving it.
  Sources: https://spectrumlocalnews.com/hi/hawaii/news/2023/11/29/paeahu-solar-project-agreement-terminated ;
  https://www.hawaiianelectric.com/hawaiian-electric-innergex-end-contract-for-planned-grid-scale-solar-battery-storage-in-south-maui

**The Paeahu lesson (central to the paper):** the developer *won every merits contest*
(PUC approval, Supreme Court affirmance) and the project still died. Opposition did not
have to prevail on the merits — the **delay** it injected (county-permit remand + appeal)
collided with post-COVID cost inflation and killed the project. The intervention right is
a **delay weapon**, and delay alone was sufficient.

### Kahana Solar (Maui) — opposition bought off, then economics finished it

20 MW + 80 MWh on Maui Land & Pineapple land, West Maui; Innergex; MECO. **West Maui
Preservation Association** intervened in the PPA docket → **PUC-ordered mediation** →
**Oct 2021 settlement: $1.375M (~$55k/yr × 25 yr) community-benefits package + a covenant
not to oppose future permits**; WMPA withdrew and the PUC approved. Counsel: Ryan Hurley +
Bianca Isaki for WMPA. The project was then **withdrawn 2022-12-30 for "pricing and supply
chain issues"** — an economic cancellation, not an opposition block. (Date correction: not
May 2022 as earlier notes had it.)
Sources: https://www.civilbeat.org/2021/11/how-a-maui-solar-farm-reached-an-unprecedented-deal-with-neighbors/ ;
https://www.mauinews.com/news/local-news/2023/04/more-energy-projects-needed-to-replace-retiring-power-sources/

### Oahu at the PUC stage — a clean NULL

**No Oahu solar PPA docket drew community intervention or a contested case.** Oahu's
Stage-1/2 setbacks (Kupehau, Mahi, Barbers Point) were developer economics or on-site
cultural resources — never a PUC-stage community fight. This mirrors the LUC's
0-intervenor record. Source: https://puc.hawaii.gov/energy/renewable-energy-procurement/by-island/

## Stage 3 — County land-use + community opposition (Maui, not Oahu)

The county planning-commission SUP/SMA hearing precedes the LUC and is the venue where, on
Maui, organized community groups actually stopped a project (Paeahu, above). **On Oahu this
stage is a documented NULL for solar.** Sweeping every Oahu utility-scale solar project —
Central Oahu (Mahi, Hoʻohana, Clearway Mililani, Kupehau), West Oahu/Kapolei (AES West
Oahu, Kupono, Barbers Point/Kalaeloa), North Shore (Kawailoa), Leeward (Waianae) — turned
up **no organized community group, no neighborhood-board opposition resolution, no
contested-case intervention, and no lawsuit against a solar project.**

Where Oahu "opposition" appears at all it is (a) **individual** — ~16 scattered emails and
Dr. Kioni Dudley's testimony at AES West Oahu SP21-411 (LUC approved anyway); (b)
**cultural-preservation, explicitly not anti-solar** — Ewa Beach historian **John Bond**
asked Innergex to reconfigure Barbers Point around the WWII Ewa Field battlefield, stating
"I'm not trying to stop the solar farm" (Historic Hawaiʻi Foundation cautiously
supportive); or (c) **legislative/lobby-level** — the HB 593 (2019) fight over putting
solar on A-rated prime ag land drew DOA/Office of Planning/Farm Bureau/Sierra Club/Life of
the Land opposition, an ag-policy fight, not a neighborhood one. None delayed or blocked a
project.
Sources: https://www.staradvertiser.com/2021/10/25/hawaii-news/solar-farm-planned-amid-cultural-and-historic-sites-including-wwii-ewa-battlefield/ ;
https://luc.hawaii.gov/completed-dockets/special-permits/oahu/sp21-411/ ;
https://www.staradvertiser.com/2019/03/25/hawaii-news/bill-to-put-solar-farms-on-prime-ag-land-elicits-stiff-opposition/

**Oahu's mass-mobilization energy went to WIND, not solar** (Kahuku/Na Pua Makani, ~200
arrests; Keep the North Shore Country litigation). Kahuku wind protesters publicly said
they would have supported a solar farm instead.
Source: https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2019-10-17/kahuku-wind-farm-protesters-want-a-solar-farm-instead

The opposition attorney bar tracked in `legal-representation-map.md` (Collins, Isaki,
Lizzi) appears **only on Maui**. The contrast is not "Oahu opposes solar less than Maui" —
it is that **organized solar opposition on Oahu is absent from the documented record
entirely.**

### Neighbor-island contrast in one number

Of **five Maui grid-scale solar+storage projects the PUC approved 2018–2023, four were
cancelled and only one — AES Kūihelani (60 MW) — was built.** The four dead: **Paeahu**
(Innergex, opposition-delay + economics), **Kahana** (Innergex, economics after CBA
settlement), **Pulehu** (Longroad, 40 MW, economics), **Kamaʻole Solar** (Potentia/Peg
Gen, 40 MW, economics). HECO's VP called the pattern "concerning."
Sources: https://www.staradvertiser.com/2023/11/29/hawaii-news/nixed-solar-farm-projects-on-maui-raising-concerns/ ;
https://www.mauinews.com/news/local-news/2023/04/more-energy-projects-needed-to-replace-retiring-power-sources/
(An "80% attrition / 4 of 5" phrasing also circulates but the widely-quoted version traces
to a reader comment — cite the Star-Advertiser body text, which names the four projects.)

Other islands: Hawaiʻi Island's one organic solar fight is the **Ocean View / Hawaiian
Ranchos** FIT-subdivision case (self-represented residents Ann & Peter Bosted, ~600-sig
petition, PUC complaint) — a D/E-land residential-subdivision matter,
different from utility-scale-on-farmland. Kauaʻi's **West Kauai Energy Project** litigation
(Pōʻai Wai Ola/Nā Kiaʻi Kai, Earthjustice) targeted the **hydro/water-diversion**
component, **not** the PV.
Sources: https://www.civilbeat.org/2016/08/does-this-big-island-community-really-need-30000-solar-panels/ ;
https://earthjustice.org/press/2024/community-groups-reach-legal-settlement-over-controversial-kauai-energy-project

## Stage 4 — Interconnection / grid capacity (a real delay driver; occasionally fatal)

Interconnection is the confidential-but-real bottleneck. Its role in outright *mortality*
is smaller than procurement economics, but it is a documented **delay** cause and a named
**cancellation** cause on the neighbor islands, and it feeds the cost increases that kill
projects at Stage 1.

- The PUC established an **Interconnection Dispute Resolution Process (IDRP), Order No.
  39163, 2023-04-18**, explicitly "in light of recent delays that projects in the Stage 1
  and 2 RFPs encountered" with interconnection study assumptions, equipment requirements,
  and cost allocation. The PUC also commissioned **PA Consulting** to study interconnection
  timeliness and cost (Phase 2 report 2023-12-28). Direct evidence that interconnection was
  a systemic Stage-1/2 delay source.
  Source: https://puc.hawaii.gov/energy/renewable-energy-procurement/stage-3/
- **ENGIE's Puakō Solar (Hawaiʻi Island, 60 MW), abandoned Nov 2021, cited "high
  interconnection costs" first** among its reasons (with supply chain + tariffs).
  Source: https://www.utilitydive.com/news/engie-abandons-hawaii-solar-storage-project-supply-chain-tariff-con/609361/
- Structural context (`notes/oahu-grid-public-record.md`): North Shore/Wahiawa renewables
  reach the grid only via **46 kV feeders at capacity**; there is no 138 kV north of the
  central spine; moving REZ Zone 8 (>1 GW north-shore potential) south costs ~$1.2–1.3 B.
  HECO's July 2026 prioritization of contained zones (Kunia/Schofield, Koolaupoko) over the
  north shore is consistent with interconnection cost gating where projects can even be
  proposed. This shapes the procurement map *before* a developer bids — an upstream filter
  more than a kill stage.
- No Oahu solar project in the record was killed *solely* by interconnection; it operates
  as a cost-and-delay amplifier that pushes marginal projects over the economic cliff at
  Stage 1.

## Stage 5 — Land / lease control (a NULL)

No Oahu (or Hawaii) utility-scale solar project in the record died over land control or a
lease dispute. Consistent with the project's scarcity-rent framing: large legacy
landholders (Kamehameha Schools, Castle & Cooke, Robinson/Gay & Robinson, Monsanto/Hartung,
Mahi Pono, ranches) are **lessors who monetize siting rights**, not obstacles — they appear
FOR projects, never against. The one land-adjacent Oahu wrinkle is beneficial, not fatal:
projects on **federal land** (Kupono on Navy JBPHH) or **Urban-district** land (Hoʻohana via
boundary-amendment A92-683) bypass Chapter 205 friction entirely. Land is where solar
*avoids* death, not where it dies. (Search for lease-dispute cancellations returned none.)

## The ranked answer: where does Oahu solar die?

1. **HECO's competitive procurement is where Oahu solar dies — by a wide margin, and as
   CANCELLATION, not blocking.** Roughly 5 of 8 Stage-2 Oahu solar awards, ≥1 Stage-3
   award, and 3 pre-RFP farms failed here; the causes are supply-chain/inflation
   (2021–22), then HECO's post-wildfire credit distress (2024), then price disputes. The
   pivotal actor is **HECO itself** as monopsony offtaker holding a unilateral
   termination lever (the 2016 SunEdison episode, which PUC staff condemned, is the
   cleanest illustration).
2. **Interconnection cost/delay is the strong second** — rarely the sole cause of death,
   but the documented reason the PUC built the IDRP, a named factor in a neighbor-island
   cancellation (Puakō), and the amplifier that pushes marginal projects over the Stage-1
   economic cliff. Largely invisible because ratings are confidential.
3. **PUC PPA approval and county/community opposition are, ON OAHU, near-zero mortality.**
   The PUC almost never denies a solar PPA, and no organized community group has
   contested an Oahu solar project at any venue. This is the honest, and somewhat
   surprising, finding: **the "community blocks solar" story is a MAUI story, not an Oahu
   story.** The one Oahu project ever stopped by anything permit-like (Kupehau) was halted
   by on-site cultural resources, not by an organized group.
4. **Land/lease: not a mortality stage at all** — landholders enable projects; they do not block them.

### The hardest / most-blocking stage, stated plainly

- **On the merits (a formal veto): NO stage reliably blocks Oahu solar.** The PUC approves,
  the courts affirm (Paeahu won at the Supreme Court), the LUC is unanimous, landholders
  cooperate. There is no venue where an Oahu solar project is reliably *defeated*.
- **In practice (what actually removes projects): HECO procurement**, via cancellation for
  economic/credit reasons. This is the binding mortality stage for Oahu.
- **The one place opposition genuinely kills a project — Maui's county-permit tier — works
  by DELAY**, not by winning on the merits; the delay becomes fatal only when it coincides with a cost
  shock (Paeahu). That mechanism has **no Oahu instance.**

### Oahu vs. neighbor islands

- **Oahu:** solar mortality is ~100% economic/procurement (HECO cancellations, supply
  chain, credit). Zero organized opposition; zero contested PPAs; zero permit denials.
- **Maui:** the same economic mortality PLUS a genuine, professionalized community-legal
  opposition layer (Pono Power, WMPA; Collins/Isaki/Lizzi) that killed Paeahu by attrition
  and monetized Kahana via a CBA. 4 of 5 Maui PUC-approved solar projects died.
- **Kauaʻi/Hawaiʻi Island:** opposition, where it exists, targets adjacent
  hydro/water-diversion (West Kauai) or FIT residential subdivisions (Ocean View) — not
  utility-scale-on-farmland solar.

## Honest evidence-quality caveats

- The **~63% Stage-2 Oahu attrition** figure is my reconstruction from HECO's award list
  vs. the current active list, not a published HECO/PUC number. Kaukonahua and Mehana
  cancellation dates/reasons are **UNVERIFIED** (they simply vanish from the active list).
- The 2016 SunEdison termination and the Stage-2/3 cancellations are well-sourced to
  Environment Hawaii, Civil Beat, Star-Advertiser, HPR, and E&E News.
- Interconnection's mortality role is the **thinnest** quantitatively — per-project
  interconnection costs are confidential; I infer its importance from the IDRP's stated
  rationale and one explicit neighbor-island cancellation (Puakō), not from an Oahu
  project killed solely by it.
- The **Oahu community-opposition NULL is a strong finding** (broad multi-project sweep,
  corroborated by the 0-intervenor LUC census and the Maui-only opposition bar) but is by
  nature a negative — absence of evidence in the searched record, not proof no quiet
  objection ever existed. Neighborhood Board #34's posture on AES West Oahu and any
  unlogged county records are the residual gaps.
- Corrections made to prior notes: **Kahana cancelled 2022-12-30** (not May 2022);
  **Lizzi/Hurley were intervenor-side** (not applicant) on Paeahu's county track.
