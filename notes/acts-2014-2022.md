# Amendments to HRS §§ 205-2(d)(6) / 205-4.5(a)(20)-(21) after Act 217 (2011): Acts 52 & 55 (2014) through Act 255 (2025)

Compiled 2026-07-11. Every claim cites a primary source (Wayback Machine capture of a
capitol.hawaii.gov document, or LRB publication). Items not yet traced are marked UNVERIFIED.

## Headline structural finding

Of the nine acts amending §§ 205-2 / 205-4.5 after 2011 examined here, only the two
2014 acts (52 and 55) changed the SOLAR siting rules. The remaining amendment line —
Act 228 (2015, small hydro), Act 173 (2016, small hydro), Act 49 (2018, wireless
broadband), Act 77 (2021, ag subdivisions/CPR), Act 131 (2022, composting), Act 182
(2024, overnight camps), Act 255 (2025, waste/disposal facilities) — added OTHER uses
to the same statutes and left the solar paragraphs untouched (verified per-act below).
The solar regime in force today is the 2011 cap (Act 217) plus
the 2014 SUP overlay (Act 55), with Act 52's class-A road exception having sunset on
2019-06-30.

Provenance of the SUP clause (open question ANSWERED): the clause
"unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6" in § 205-2(d)(6)(B)
was NOT in Act 217 (2011) — the enacted SB631 CD1 text of (d)(6)(B) ends
"...or twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser;" with no SUP language
(source: https://web.archive.org/web/*/https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/session2011/bills/SB631_CD1_.HTM,
local cache data/raw/capitol/SB631_CD1_.HTM). It was inserted by ACT 55 (2014,
SB2658): the § 205-2(d)(6)(B) clause first appeared in SD2 (Senate WTL, Chair
Malama Solomon, decision 2/27/2014), on a recommendation first put on the record
by the Land Use Commission (Daniel Orodenker) on 2/6/2014; the parallel
"or for which a special use permit is granted" in § 205-4.5(a)(20) first appeared
in HD1 (House AGR, Chair Jessica Wooley, 3/13/2014); and the separate SUP category
§ 205-4.5(a)(21) — with the 50%-below-fair-market-rent compatible-ag lease,
financial-security, and decommissioning/restoration conditions — was created in
HD2 (House EEP/WAL, Chairs Chris Lee / Cindy Evans, 3/27/2014). Act 52 (2014,
SB2775) used a SUP only inside its class-A road exception, § 205-4.5(a)(20)(C),
which sunset 6/30/2019. Neither Act 131 (2022, composting) nor Act 255 (2025,
waste facilities) touched the clause; both carry it forward verbatim.
Full version-by-version documentation in the per-act sections below.

### 4. Introduced-vs-enacted delta on siting rules

#### Introduced bill (SB2775, 1/23/2014)

Amended BOTH § 205-2(d) and § 205-4.5(a):

1. New § 205-2(d)(7): "Notwithstanding section 205-4.5 to the contrary, solar energy facilities placed within land with soil classified as overall productivity rating class A; provided that the facilities: (A) Shall not occupy more than **five per cent of the acreage of the parcel, or five acres of land, whichever is lesser**; (B) Shall not materially interfere with farm operations; and (C) Shall cause no more than a minimal adverse impact on existing or potential agricultural uses of the land;"
2. § 205-4.5(a)(20) amended so the class-A ban is qualified: "...productivity rating class A[;] **unless the solar energy facilities do not materially interfere with farm operations and cause no more than a minimal adverse impact on existing or potential agricultural uses of the land**; or"
3. No sunset; effective on approval.
4. Left existing § 205-2(d)(6) untouched — as reproduced in the introduced bill, (d)(6)(B) read: "Solar energy facilities placed within land with soil classified as overall productivity rating class B or C shall not occupy more than ten per cent of the acreage of the parcel, or twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser;" — **no SUP clause present** (consistent with 2011 Act 217 text).

#### SD1 (Senate AGL/ENE, 2/27–2/28/2014)

Committees "amended this measure by deleting its contents and inserting" new language (SSCR2607). SD1:
- **Dropped the § 205-2 amendment entirely** (no more 5%/5-acre class-A carve-out).
- Rewrote only § 205-4.5(a)(20): class-A ban "...class A[;] **unless the solar energy facilities are located on a paved or unpaved road in existence as of December 31, 2013, and are placed in a manner that still allows vehicular traffic to use the road**; or"
- Rationale (SSCR2607): "no prime agricultural land will be sacrificed for solar energy facilities" — a "compromise" limiting class-A solar to field roads. The road-exception language was proposed verbatim by HARC's testimony ("in consultation with various senators, we now suggest the following language").
- SSCR2607 flags forward: "your Committees request consideration of the Land Use Commission's testimony to subject solar energy facilities on class A, B, and C agricultural lands to the state special permit process."
- Still no sunset.

#### HD1 (House AGR, 3/17–3/21/2014) — final enacted text

§ 205-4.5(a)(20) class-A exception restructured into three cumulative conditions:

> "...productivity rating class A[;] unless the solar energy facilities are:
> (A) Located on a paved or unpaved road in existence as of December 31, 2013, and the parcel of land upon which the paved or unpaved road is located has a valid county agriculture tax dedication status or a valid agricultural conservation easement;
> (B) Placed in a manner that still allows vehicular traffic to use the road; and
> (C) **Granted a special use permit by the commission pursuant to section 205-6**; or"

New Section 3 (sunset + grandfathering):

> "SECTION 3. This Act shall take effect upon its approval; provided that:
> (1) This Act shall be repealed on **June 30, 2019**, and section 205-4.5, Hawaii Revised Statutes, shall be reenacted in the form in which it read on the day before the effective date of this Act; and
> (2) Any solar energy facility permitted under this Act as of June 30, 2019, shall continue to be permissible under the provisions of this Act until the end of its operable life, at which time it shall be appropriately and properly replaced or decommissioned and removed within twelve months."

Provenance of HD1 conditions (per HSCR1107-14 and testimony):
- Condition (A) tax-dedication/easement language: proposed by **HDOA (Scott Enright)** in 3/17 AGR testimony (draft language quoted there).
- Condition (C) SUP requirement: proposed by **LUC (Daniel Orodenker)** with draft language "and a special use permit has been granted by the land use commission pursuant to section 205-6," and endorsed by **DBEDT (Richard Lim)**; OP made supportive comments.
- Sunset/decommissioning: HD1 addition; HARC's 3/17 testimony noted "we also understand that there is a desire to have a sunset added to this proposed bill and are not in opposition."

#### KEY ANSWER — SUP-clause provenance question

**SB2775/Act 52 did NOT insert "unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6" into § 205-2(d)(6)(B), and did NOT insert a general "or for which a special use permit is granted" SUP path into § 205-4.5(a)(20) for B/C soils.** The enacted SD1 HD1 amends ONLY § 205-4.5(a)(20) and only for the class-A road exception (SUP appears solely as new subparagraph (a)(20)(C) applying to that class-A exception). SD1 deleted the bill's § 205-2 section entirely. The general B/C-soils SUP language in current §§ 205-2(d)(6)(B) and 205-4.5(a)(20) therefore came from **2014 Act 55 (likely vehicle: SB2658 SD3 HD1, flagged by LUC testimony as the parallel bill amending the same sections)** — UNVERIFIED pending pull of SB2658/Act 55. Note also: the status page's act description for SB2775 is class-A-only; project CLAUDE.md's line that Act 52 "amended §§ 205-2, 205-4.5(a)(20)" is contradicted by the enacted HD1 text (only § 205-4.5(a) amended) — correct this in the master notes.

### 6. Who pushed to TIGHTEN vs LOOSEN

- LOOSEN (open class A, narrowly): HARC (the bill's evident proponent, with a specific denied Kunia PV project on its own conservation-easement land), Hawaii Cattlemen's Council, Hawaii Farm Bureau (support votes, no asks), DLNR, four individuals. HARC also pushed to strip the SUP condition and the mandatory decommissioning clause at the last hearing (unsuccessfully).
- TIGHTEN / add process: LUC (SUP for A/B/C — partially adopted, A-only), DBEDT (endorsed SUP), HDOA (tax-dedication/easement condition — adopted; steer solar to D/E lands), OP (cautionary comments, "impermanence syndrome").
- Restructure (neither): DPP Honolulu (performance standards in § 205-6; would have LOOSENED C-lands to permitted use while keeping SUP on A/B).
- This bill is a second counterexample to the astroturf hypothesis in the same spirit as HB 2665 (2018): the push to LOOSEN came from an agricultural research nonprofit and ranchers; the push to TIGHTEN came from state agencies. No utility, large-landowner trade association, or solar-industry presence in the record.

### Version-by-version delta on siting rules

Baseline (2011 Act 217 law): solar permitted on B/C only up to **10% of parcel or 20 acres, whichever is lesser**; banned on class A; **no SUP clause of any kind**.

#### Introduced (1/17/2014) — LOOSENING
§ 205-2(d)(6)(B): B/C solar "shall not occupy more than [ten] **thirty per cent** of the acreage of the parcel, or [twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser;]**, in the alternative, of two or more adjacent parcels** having met the applicable county requirements for the joint development or joint lot use of those parcels; **provided that the area occupied by the solar energy facilities in excess of twenty acres shall only be made available for agricultural activities that are compatible with the solar energy facilities; provided further that the solar energy facilities shall be decommissioned and removed within twelve months of the conclusion of operation**". Parallel change in § 205-4.5(a)(20). NO special use permit. NO rent condition. (Decommissioning-in-12-months was in the bill from day one.)

#### SD1 (ENE/AGL, 2/6 hearing; SSCR2217) — adds the below-market-rent condition
Kept 30% / deleted-20-acre-cap structure, but the excess-of-20-acres area must now be made available for compatible ag "**at a lease rate that is at least fifty per cent below fair market rent for comparable properties**". SSCR2217 amendment item (1). This responds directly to First Wind's 2/6 request (see testimony). Still NO SUP.

#### SD2 (WTL, 2/19 hearing, 2/27 decision; SSCR2609) — inserts the SUP requirement; reverts the cap; adds sunset
- § 205-2(d)(6)(B): reverted to "**ten per cent... or twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser[;], unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6**; provided that: (i) the area occupied ... is also made available for compatible agricultural activities at a lease rate that is at least fifty per cent below the fair market rent for comparable properties ... and (ii) ... decommissioned and removed within twelve months".
- § 205-4.5(a)(20): struck the 10%/20-acre cap entirely; (20) became solar "**for which a special use permit has been granted pursuant to section 205-6**" with (A) 50%-below-FMR ag lease on the occupied area (no longer just the excess over 20 acres), (B) decommissioning, (C) class-A ban.
- Added **sunset: repeal July 1, 2020** with reenactment of prior law.
- SSCR2609 (Chair Solomon): "Your Committee's utmost concern is to preserve the agricultural use of prime agricultural land... your Committee requests the Land Use Commission to build in adequate conditions into the special use permit to ensure that agricultural activities are genuinely compatible ... as well as to protect against agricultural land being used as a 'gentleman's farm'."

#### SD3 (Senate Floor Amendment No. 7, adopted 3/4) — re-loosens cap, SUP for everything
§ 205-2(d)(6)(B): "shall not occupy more than [ten] **thirty per cent** of the acreage of the parcel, or twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser[;]**, or in the alternative, of two or more adjacent parcels** ...; provided that: (i) **A special use permit has been granted** ...; (ii) [50%-below-FMR lease]; and (iii) [decommissioning]". Same restructuring in (a)(20) as (A)–(D). I.e., 30%-or-20-acre cap plus adjacent-parcel alternative, with SUP + rent condition applying to ALL B/C solar. Sunset retained (SFA7 did not touch section 5).

#### HD1 (House AGR, 3/13 hearing; HSCR1174-14) — final architecture: cap OR SUP path
- § 205-2(d)(6)(B): "shall not occupy more than **ten per cent** of the acreage of the parcel, or **twenty acres of land, whichever is lesser**[;], **unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6 and** the area occupied ... is also made available for compatible agricultural activities **at a lease rate that is at least fifty per cent below the fair market rent** ...; provided further that solar energy facilities for which a special use permit has been granted shall be decommissioned and removed within twelve months...".
- § 205-4.5(a)(20): "Solar energy facilities that do not occupy more than ten per cent ... or twenty acres ..., whichever is lesser[;], **or for which a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6**; provided that: (A) A special use permit shall not be granted ... unless the area occupied ... [50% below FMR]; (B) [decommission 12 mo.]; and (C) [class A ban]".
- **Deleted the adjacent-parcels/joint-development language** (LUC had "strongly recommend[ed]" its removal) and **deleted the July 1, 2020 sunset** (HSCR1174 items (2) and (4)).
- HD1 tracks nearly verbatim the proposed-HD1 draft **attached to First Wind Solar Group's 3/13 AGR testimony**.

#### HD2 (House EEP/WAL, 3/25 hearing, 3/27 decision; HSCR1578-14) — creates (a)(21); adds financial security + restoration
- § 205-2(d)(6)(B): pared to "... whichever is lesser[;], **unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6**;" (conditions moved to 205-4.5).
- § 205-4.5(a)(20): "...whichever is lesser[;] **or for which a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6**; provided that this use shall not be permitted on lands with soil ... class A;"
- **NEW § 205-4.5(a)(21):** "Solar energy facilities on lands with soil classified ... rating B or C **for which a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6**; provided that: (A) The area occupied ... is also made available for compatible agricultural activities **at a lease rate that is at least fifty per cent below the fair market rent** for comparable properties; **(B) Proof of financial security to decommission the facility is provided to the satisfaction of the appropriate county planning commission prior to date of commencement of commercial generation; and (C) Solar energy facilities shall be decommissioned at the owner's expense** according to the following requirements: (i) Removal of all equipment ... within twelve months of the conclusion of operation or useful life; and (ii) **Restoration of the disturbed earth to substantially the same physical condition** as existed prior to the development..." Geothermal renumbered [(21)] (22).
- HSCR1578: committees added the financial-security proof and "[d]ecommissioning **similar to that currently required under Hawaii law for wind energy facilities**". (First Wind's attached proposed-HD2 draft did NOT contain the financial-security or restoration clauses — those were the committees' own addition, going beyond any testifier's written ask.)
- No sunset in HD2 → Act 55 is permanent (unlike companion-in-spirit Act 52/SB2775, which sunset 6/30/2019).

#### Definitive answers to the key questions
1. **Yes** — SB2658 (Act 55) inserted "unless a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6" into § 205-2(d)(6)(B). First appeared in **SD2 (WTL committee, Chair Malama Solomon, decision 2/27/2014)**.
2. **Yes** — it inserted "or for which a special use permit is granted pursuant to section 205-6" into § 205-4.5(a)(20). That exact formulation first appeared in **HD1 (House AGR, Chair Jessica Wooley, 3/13/2014)** (SD2 had briefly made (a)(20) SUP-only).
3. **Yes** — it created the new **§ 205-4.5(a)(21)** SUP category (50%-below-FMR ag lease + financial security + owner-expense decommissioning + earth restoration). The (a)(21) split, the financial-security condition, and the restoration requirement were all created in **HD2 (House EEP/WAL, Chairs Chris Lee and Cindy Evans, decision 3/27/2014)**.
4. **The 50%-below-fair-market-rent condition first entered in SD1 (Senate ENE/AGL, Chairs Gabbard/Nishihara, 2/6/2014 hearing)**, applying then only to the area in excess of 20 acres; SD2 extended it to the entire SUP-permitted area. Provenance: **First Wind Solar Group requested a "below-market lease rent" condition in its 2/6/2014 testimony** (its own proposed text said only "below market rent"; the "at least fifty per cent" figure first appears in the committee's SD1). By 2/19 First Wind's late WTL testimony proposed the exact "at least fifty per cent below the fair market rent" + SUP package, crediting drafting assistance from the **Land Use Commission and the Sierra Club**.
5. **The SUP idea was first put on the record by the Land Use Commission (Daniel E. Orodenker, Executive Officer)** at the 2/6/2014 ENE/AGL hearing ("We would recommend that the measure be modified to require that solar energy facilities proposed for LSB class A, B, or C lands go through the State Special Permit process") and again at WTL on 2/19 with draft statutory text nearly identical to what SD2 adopted.
6. Looser/tighter versions: introduced = loosest (30% cap, no 20-acre limit, no SUP, no rent condition); SD2 = tightest cap-side (10%/20ac + SUP + 50% rent + 2020 sunset); SD3 = looser again (30%/20ac + adjacent parcels, SUP for all); HD1/HD2 = enacted compromise (old 10%/20ac permitted as-of-right, unlimited-size via SUP with rent/decommissioning conditions, no sunset).

## HB247 (2021) — Act 77, SLH 2021 — "RELATING TO AGRICULTURAL LANDS" (subdivision/CPR cleanup, NOT a solar bill)

### Identity
- HB247 SD2; Act 077, signed 06/24/2021 (Gov. Msg. No. 1179).
- Report title: "Agricultural Lands; Land Subdivisions; Condominium Property Regime". Companion: SB340.
- Purpose (Section 1): "to amend certain land subdivision and condominium property regime laws related to agricultural land, as recommended by the office of planning in its study of subdivision and condominium property regimes on agricultural lands on Oahu conducted pursuant to Act 278, Session Laws of Hawaii 2019."

### Sponsors
TARNAS (lead), BRANCO, HASHIMOTO, HOLT, ICHIYAMA, ILAGAN, LOPRESTI, LOWEN, MARTEN, MATAYOSHI, MATSUMOTO, MCKELVEY, MORIKAWA, NAKAMURA, NAKASHIMA, OKIMOTO, PERRUSO, QUINLAN, TODD, WILDBERGER, YAMASHITA.

### Committee path
House: WAL (passed unamended 2/2/21) -> AGR (passed unamended 2/10/21) -> JHA (passed unamended 2/24/21) -> Third Reading 3/4/21.
Senate: AEN/WTL (SD1, 3/22/21) -> CPN/WAM (SD2, 4/7/21) -> Third Reading 4/13/21. House initially disagreed, then reconsidered and agreed to SD2 on 4/22/21 (no conference). Act 77 on 6/24/21.

### Did it change solar siting language? NO.
The SD2 (enacted) text reprints all of HRS 205-4.5(a) because it amends subsection (a), but the only change inside (a) is in paragraph (4): the "farm dwelling" definition changes "located on and [used in connection with] accessory to a farm". Solar paragraphs (a)(20) and (a)(21) are reproduced verbatim as existing law (10%/20-acre cap; class A ban; SUP on B/C soils with 50%-below-FMR lease, decommissioning security, restoration) with no bracketed or underscored material. The phrase "and for solar energy facilities, class B or C" in the (a) preamble is pre-existing law, not new.
Other operative changes: 205-4.5(f) (subdivided/leased ag lots — county enforcement authority and fines under 46-4, 205-12, 205-13; lease-term provisions) and 514B-52(b) (condo-property-regime registration in the ag district requires verified county-official statement of chapter 205 compliance). No amendment to 205-2(d)(6). Effective 7/1/2021.
Introduced version had the same scope (same 6 "solar" mentions, all in reprinted existing text); Senate drafts did not add solar language.

# FAILED MEASURE: SB942 (2021) — BOA attestation chokepoint

## SB942 (2021) — "RELATING TO AGRICULTURAL LANDS" (BOA attestation chokepoint for solar PPAs)

### Identity
- Bill: SB942, 31st Legislature, Regular Session of 2021. No act number — never enacted.
- Report title: "Board of Agriculture; Public Utilities Commission; Solar Energy Facility; Letter of Attestation; Power Purchase Agreements"
- Companion: none (status page "Companion:" field blank).

### Sponsors
GABBARD (lead), CHANG, INOUYE, MISALUCHA, RIVIERE, Ihara, Moriwaki, Shimabukuro (all Senate).
Note (revealed conduct only): Sen. Mike Gabbard chaired the Senate Committee on Agriculture and Environment (AEN) in 2021, the lead committee of referral; the bill was nonetheless never scheduled for a hearing.

### Committee path / status (complete history)
- 1/27/2021 S: Introduced; Passed First Reading.
- 1/29/2021 S: Referred to AEN/EET (joint), then CPN.
- 12/10/2021 D: Carried over to 2022 Regular Session.
- No further action in 2022. Post-sine-die capture (2022-07-10) shows: "Committee Reports: No other committee reports available"; "Testimony: No testimony available at this time"; "Hearing Notices: No hearings scheduled"; "All Versions of this Measure: SB942" (introduced version only).
- FATE: DIED IN COMMITTEE (AEN/EET) WITHOUT EVER RECEIVING A HEARING, in both 2021 and the 2022 carryover year.

### What it would have done (from introduced text, SB942_.pdf, 2021-0154 SB SMA.doc)
1. New HRS chapter 141 section: Board of Agriculture (BOA) authority to "Issue or not issue a letter of attestation", "Set conditions for the issuance", "Refuse to issue a letter of attestation if such conditions are not accepted", "Compel compliance ... throughout the term of a power purchase agreement", and "Require further information from solar energy facility developers or landowners, as needed."
2. New HRS chapter 269 (PUC) section: "The public utilities commission shall not approve a power purchase agreement for a solar energy facility on lands with soil classified by the land study bureau's detailed land classification as overall (master) productivity rating B or C unless the board of agriculture submits a letter to the commission ... that attests the following: (1) That a solar energy facility is the best use of that particular piece of land to help the State attain its goals of sustainability; (2) The solar energy facility is not displacing or causing to be evicted any agricultural operation that has been active in the past eighteen months; (3) The landowner or solar developer is helping new agricultural operations to be established elsewhere in the State; and (4) The landowner or solar developer is taking steps to ensure that compatible agriculture coexists with the solar energy facility from the commencement ... through the termination of operations..."
3. Amendments to HRS 205-4.5(a)(21) (SUP solar on B/C soils), tightening the ag-concession conditions:
   - (A): area occupied by facilities ["is also made available for" -> struck] "shall be utilized for economically viable compatible agricultural activities ... no later than the commencement of full operation of the facility";
   - (B): "The lease rate [that is at least fifty (per cent below) -> struck] to the compatible agricultural activity shall not exceed seventy-five per cent below the fair market rent for comparable properties" (i.e., rent capped at 25% of FMR, cheaper for the farmer than current law's "at least fifty per cent below");
   - new decommissioning-security and removal/restoration clauses renumbered.
   - Effective upon approval.
- Design: a per-project chokepoint routed through PPA approval — every utility-scale solar project on B/C ag soils would have needed a discretionary BOA letter before the PUC could approve its PPA, with BOA able to impose and enforce conditions for the life of the PPA.
