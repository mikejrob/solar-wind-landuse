# Community Resistance to Renewable-Energy Projects in Hawaii — an Island-Organized Catalogue

Standing reference section. Compiled 2026-07-19. Companion data: `data/community_resistance_cases.csv`;
downloaded primary sources: `sources/community-resistance/` (by island) with `MANIFEST.md`.

**Scope.** Every documented instance of *organized* community resistance — neighborhood/community
associations, coalitions, Native Hawaiian/cultural groups, environmental NGOs acting as opposition,
street protest/arrests, contested-case intervention, litigation, or ordinance-driving petitions —
against a utility-scale **solar, wind, storage, or biomass** project, OR against a permissive siting
bill, in Hawaii. The legislative forum (testimony campaigns, ordinance drives) is included as a venue
of resistance. All six inhabited islands covered. *Individual* testimony and purely *economic*
project deaths are noted but distinguished from organized resistance, and absences (nulls) are
reported as findings.

**Evidentiary discipline.** Every claim is URL-cited or flagged UNVERIFIED. Court opinions, the
Honolulu ordinance, and the West Kauai environmental record are downloaded locally; paywalled/
Cloudflare/JS-gated sources (Civil Beat, Star-Advertiser, Maui News, PUC Salesforce CDMS) are
retained as links. Revealed conduct only — who protested, intervened, testified, litigated — never
inferred intent.

## The cross-island pattern (read this first)

**Organized resistance to utility-scale renewables in Hawaii is overwhelmingly a WIND and a
NEIGHBOR-ISLAND story, not a solar or an Oahu-generation story.**

1. **The solar/wind split.** The mass-mobilization, street-protest, and multi-year litigation energy
   attaches to **wind** (Kahuku/Na Pua Makani on Oahu; "Big Wind" on Lanai and Molokai) and to
   **biomass** (Hu Honua on Hawaii Island). Organized opposition to *utility-scale solar* is rare and
   confined almost entirely to **Maui** (Paeahu, Kahana). Kahuku wind protesters said explicitly they
   would have welcomed a solar farm instead.

2. **Which islands see organized solar resistance, which don't.**
   - **Maui** — the *only* island with a professionalized community-legal opposition layer aimed at
     solar (Pono Power Coalition, West Maui Preservation Association; the Collins/Isaki/Lizzi/Hurley
     public-interest bar). It killed Paeahu (by delay) and monetized Kahana (by CBA).
   - **Oahu** — a **strong NULL for solar**: no organized group, no neighborhood-board resolution, no
     contested-case intervention, no lawsuit against any Oahu utility-scale solar project was found.
     Oahu's mobilization went to wind. Oahu solar dies of economics inside HECO procurement.
   - **Kauai** — resistance targets the **water-diversion/hydro** component of a hybrid project
     (West Kauai Energy Project), never the PV; solar SUPs drew zero intervenors.
   - **Hawaii Island** — the organized fights are **biomass** (Hu Honua, killed by Life of the Land
     litigation) and a **residential-subdivision** solar-density dispute (Ocean View); no
     utility-scale-on-farmland solar fight.
   - **Lanai / Molokai** — organized opposition exists but is aimed at **Big Wind + the interisland
     cable** (Friends of Lanai, Lanaians for Sensible Growth, I Aloha Molokai), framed around
     neighbor islands bearing burdens for Oahu's benefit.

3. **The sharpest contrast in one line:** on **Oahu**, ~165–200 arrests and three appellate cases
   fought a *wind* farm while *not a single* organized group contested a *solar* project; on **Maui**,
   the reverse texture — no comparable wind fight, but a standing community-legal bar that made solar
   permitting a contested-case gauntlet.

4. **How resistance actually bites.** Almost never on the merits. The community *lost every merits
   ruling* it litigated (Na Pua Makani ×3; Paeahu at the PUC and Supreme Court). Where resistance
   changed outcomes it did so by (a) **delay** that collided with cost shocks (Paeahu), (b) a
   **mediated buy-out** (Kahana CBA), (c) **the legislative/ordinance forum** (Honolulu Ord 25-2,
   which will retire Oahu's wind fleet), or (d) **litigation on a narrow environmental hook** where
   the agency record was thin (Hu Honua GHG/public-trust; West Kauai EA).

## OAHU

### 1. Na Pua Makani Wind Farm (Kahuku) — the mass-protest case
- **Location / project:** Kahuku, North Shore; 8 turbines (tip height 568 ft), ~24–27.6 MW.
- **Developer / tech:** AES / Na Pua Makani Power Partners LLC; **wind**.
- **Resisting groups:** **Keep the North Shore Country (KNSC)**; **Kahuku Community Association**
  (Sunny Unga); North Shore/Kahuku residents.
- **Counsel:** community — **Lance D. Collins + Bianca K. Isaki**. Applicant — Carlsmith Ball
  (Manaut/Thoene) and Yamamoto Caliboso (Yamamoto/Smith/Dixon).
- **Forums / mechanisms:** (a) **street protest** — a monthslong 2019 road blockade with
  **~165–200 arrests** ("more than 160," Civil Beat 2021-06; "200," Hurley testimony); (b) **BLNR
  contested case** challenging the habitat conservation plan / incidental-take license for the
  Hawaiian hoary bat (ʻōpeʻapeʻa); (c) three appellate cases.
- **Outcome / status:** community **lost every merits ruling**. Hawaii Supreme Court **affirmed** the
  HCP/take license, **SCAP-19-0000449, 2022-02-25** (downloaded). ICA **affirmed** dismissal of the
  permit/waiver/ZBA challenge, **CAAP-21-0000396 (SDO), 2025-03-27** (downloaded). Supreme Court
  **rejected certiorari** in 2025, **SCWC-21-0000396**, over a **dissent filed 2025-07-23**
  (downloaded) arguing the petitioners had adequately asserted clean-and-healthful-environment and
  Native Hawaiian traditional/customary-practice interests. Turbines **built and operating since
  2020**.
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **unaffected** (project built; litigation exhausted 2025).
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (three court PDFs local; news links retained).
- Sources: https://law.justia.com/cases/hawaii/supreme-court/2022/scap-19-0000449.html ;
  https://caselaw.findlaw.com/court/hi-intermediate-court-of-appeals/117112435.html ;
  https://www.civilbeat.org/2021/06/this-north-shore-community-has-had-enough-of-towering-wind-turbines/
  Local: `oahu/SCAP-19-0000449.pdf`, `oahu/CAAP-21-0000396sdo.pdf`, `oahu/SCWC-21-0000396dis.pdf`.

### 2. Honolulu Ordinance 25-2 (Bill 64, 2023) — resistance winning via the ordinance forum
- **Location / project:** island-wide wind-siting rule; targets all Oahu wind (Kahuku Wind,
  Na Pua Makani, Kawailoa).
- **Resisting groups:** **Kahuku Community Association** (Sunny Unga); North Shore residents;
  attorney **Ryan D. Hurley** (testifying self).
- **Forum / mechanism:** **Honolulu City Council ordinance drive** — an escalation chain of
  community-pressure measures: **Res 19-305** (5 mi, Tsuneyoshi, adopted 2020) → **Bills 28/29/30
  (2021)** → **Bills 10/55 (2022)** → **Bill 64 (2023)** → **Ordinance 25-2**.
- **Outcome / status:** **enacted 2025-01-03.** Large wind (≥100 kW) must sit the **greater of
  10× tip height or 1.25 miles** from country/residential/apartment/resort lots; **DPP waivers
  banned**; repowering capped. **18 of 20 Kahuku turbines become nonconforming**; the Oahu wind
  fleet is expected to be **retired 2031–2040** as PPAs expire (repowering foreclosed).
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **blocks future Oahu wind island-wide** (and forecloses the existing
  fleet). The clearest instance of organized resistance *prevailing*.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (ordinance PDF local; measure page + Civil Beat retained). State analogs
  HB 2188 (2020) and SB 957 (2021) died — see `notes/wind-setbacks.md`.
- Sources: https://www4.honolulu.gov/docushare/dsweb/Get/Document-354710/Ordinance%2025-002_Bill%2064_LUO.pdf ;
  https://www.civilbeat.org/2024/06/tweaks-to-honolulus-land-use-law-could-jeopardize-kahukus-controversial-wind-farms/
  Local: `oahu/ORD25-002_Bill64_LUO.pdf`.

### 3. Kupehau Solar — a cultural-resource halt, NOT organized opposition
- Kunia, Central Oahu; 60 MW solar+storage; **174 Power Global / Hanwha**. Developer **terminated the
  PPA 2021-12-06** after **on-site cultural/archaeological sites** were found and downsizing failed.
  **No organized group** was involved — this is the one Oahu solar project ever stopped by anything
  permit/land-like, and the cause was on-site resources, not community mobilization.
- **Blocked** (by on-site cultural resources). VERIFIED (Star-Advertiser retained).
  Source: https://www.staradvertiser.com/2022/01/11/hawaii-news/oahu-renewable-energy-suffers-setback/

### 4. AES West Oahu Solar (SP21-411) — individual, not organized
- Honouliuli; 12.5 MW; AES. ~10–16 **individual** near-identical opposition emails at the county
  stage (a small coordinated email effort — but all individuals, no organization) plus Dr. Kioni
  Dudley's testimony. **LUC approved 8-0; zero intervenors.** Included to mark the boundary between
  individual testimony and organized resistance. **Unaffected.** VERIFIED.
  Source: https://luc.hawaii.gov/completed-dockets/special-permits/oahu/sp21-411/

### 5. Oahu utility-scale SOLAR generally — the STRONG NULL
- Sweeping every Oahu utility-scale solar project (Mahi, Hoʻohana, Clearway Mililani, Kupehau, AES
  West Oahu, Kupono, Barbers Point, Kawailoa, Waiawa) turned up **no organized community group, no
  neighborhood-board opposition resolution, no contested-case intervention, and no lawsuit.** Oahu
  solar mortality is ~100% economic (HECO procurement, supply chain, post-wildfire credit). The
  opposition attorney bar (Collins/Isaki/Lizzi) appears **only on Maui**. This is a negative result
  (absence in the searched record); the residual gaps are unlogged county records and Neighborhood
  Board postures. **NULL finding.** VERIFIED as a sweep.
  Sources: https://puc.hawaii.gov/energy/renewable-energy-procurement/by-island/ ;
  https://www.hawaiipublicradio.org/local-news/2019-10-17/kahuku-wind-farm-protesters-want-a-solar-farm-instead
  (See `notes/project-pipeline-mortality.md` for the full stage-by-stage mortality analysis.)

## MAUI

### 1. Paeahu Solar (South Maui) — the canonical solar-opposition case
- **Location / project:** Ulupalakua Ranch land above the Maui Meadows subdivision, South Maui;
  15 MW solar + 60 MWh storage.
- **Developer / tech:** Innergex; offtaker MECO; **solar+storage**. PUC **docket 2018-0433**.
- **Resisting groups:** **Pono Power Coalition** (Kīhei community group, 700+ members); **Maui
  Meadows Neighborhood Association**; **Maui Tomorrow Foundation**; **Hoʻoponopono O Mākena**.
- **Counsel:** **Lance D. Collins + Bianca K. Isaki** (PUC/appeal); **Christina D. Lizzi + Linda Nye**
  and **Ryan Hurley** (county intervenors). Applicant: Schlack Ito (Codiga/Ito); MECO: Kobayashi
  Sugita & Goda.
- **Forums / mechanisms:** PUC contested case (participant status granted on clean-and-healthful-
  environment grounds); **Hawaii Supreme Court appeal**; **Maui Planning Commission** SUP/Phase-II
  permits; **2nd Circuit Court**. Arguments: wildfire risk (explicit California-utility-fire analogy,
  pre-Lahaina), downslope flooding, cultural resources, endangered species.
- **Outcome / status:** developer **won on the merits** — PUC approved (D&O 37340, 2020; reconsid.
  denied Order 37553), and the **Supreme Court AFFIRMED, SCOT-21-0000041, 2022-03-02** (Eddins J.;
  Wilson J. dissenting; declined to import Sherman Act "rule of reason" into PPA review; the "common
  counsel" procurement-coordination issue is in the record). BUT **2nd Circuit Judge Kelsey Kawano
  VACATED the county permits (~late Jan 2022)** and remanded to grant intervention → hearing officer
  Glenn Kosaka; mediation failed; contested case began Nov 2022. The **PPA was jointly terminated
  2023-11-28**, citing "lengthy delays resulting from developer legal challenges along with
  pandemic-related cost and supply-chain issues."
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **KILLED — by delay.** Opposition never prevailed on the merits; the
  delay it injected collided with post-COVID cost inflation. **Only solar land-use permit ever
  vacated by a Hawaii court.** The intervention right functioned as a delay weapon.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (Supreme Court PDF local; Maui Now / Civil Beat / HECO termination
  retained).
- Sources: https://law.justia.com/cases/hawaii/supreme-court/2022/scot-21-0000041.html ;
  https://mauinow.com/2022/01/28/court-decision-delays-paeahu-solar-project/ ;
  https://www.hawaiianelectric.com/hawaiian-electric-innergex-end-contract-for-planned-grid-scale-solar-battery-storage-in-south-maui
  Local: `maui/Paeahu_SCOT-21-0000041.pdf`.

### 2. Kahana Solar (West Maui) — opposition monetized, then economics finished it
- **Location / project:** ~220 ac of Maui Land & Pineapple land, West Maui; 20 MW + 80 MWh.
- **Developer / tech:** Innergex; offtaker MECO; **solar+storage**. PUC **docket 2020-0142**.
- **Resisting group / counsel:** **West Maui Preservation Association (WMPA)**; **Ryan D. Hurley +
  Bianca K. Isaki**. Applicant: Schlack Ito.
- **Forum / mechanism:** PUC contested case → **PUC-ordered mediation** (ret. Judge Joel August).
- **Outcome / status:** **Oct 2021 settlement** — **$1.375M** (~$55k/yr × 25 yr) community-benefits
  package **+ a covenant not to oppose future permits**; WMPA withdrew; PUC approved. The project was
  then **withdrawn 2022-12-30** for "pricing and supply chain issues."
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **KILLED — by economics, after a CBA.** Opposition was *monetized*, not
  a block; economics finished the project.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (settlement PDF local; Civil Beat / Maui News retained).
- Sources: https://www.civilbeat.org/2021/11/how-a-maui-solar-farm-reached-an-unprecedented-deal-with-neighbors/
  Local: `maui/Kahana_WMPA_settlement.pdf`.

### 3. Pulehu & Kamaʻole Solar — economic deaths, NO organized opposition
- Pulehu (Longroad, 40 MW; terminated 2022-05-04) and Kamaʻole (Potentia/Peg Gen, 40 MW; among four
  Maui cancellations reported Nov 2023) died of supply-chain/inflation economics with **no organized
  opposition documented.** Of **five Maui PUC-approved solar projects 2018–2023, four died and only
  one (AES Kūihelani, 60 MW) was built** — but only **Paeahu** had organized opposition. Included to
  keep the Maui picture honest: even on Maui, most solar deaths are economic.
  Source: https://www.staradvertiser.com/2023/11/29/hawaii-news/nixed-solar-farm-projects-on-maui-raising-concerns/

### 1. Hu Honua / Honua Ola Bioenergy (Pepeekeo) — the biggest NGO litigation KILL
- **Location / project:** Pepeʻekeo, Hāmākua coast; **~21.5 MW biomass** (net; ~30 MW gross —
  exact figure **UNVERIFIED** here).
- **Developer / tech:** Hu Honua Bioenergy; offtaker HELCO; **biomass**. PUC **docket 2017-0122**.

  on remand the PUC **denied** the PPA; second appeal **SCOT-22-0000418 (2023-03-13)** **affirmed**
  the denial. The project is dead.
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **KILLED.** The single largest NGO-opposition litigation win against a
  Hawaii utility-scale renewable project. Biomass, not solar/wind, but squarely in scope.
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (both Supreme Court PDFs local).
- Sources: https://law.justia.com/cases/hawaii/supreme-court/2019/scot-17-0000630.html ;

- **Verification:** VERIFIED that the bill/testimony campaign existed (HB 2665 testimony archived);

## KAUAI

### 1. West Kauai Energy Project (Waimea) — resistance to the WATER-DIVERSION, not the PV
- **Location / project:** west Kauaʻi (Waimea River / Mānā Plain); hybrid **solar + pumped-storage
  hydro**.
- **Developer / tech:** KIUC / AES; **solar + pumped-hydro**.
- **Resisting groups / counsel:** **Pōʻai Wai Ola** and **Nā Kiaʻi Kai**; **Earthjustice.**
- **Forums / mechanisms:** challenge to the state land board's EA/FONSI (former BLNR chair signed off
  days before leaving office, without a public hearing); **Circuit Court environmental-review
  lawsuit** over the lack of an EIS and the Waimea River water diversion (a plantation-era
  water-rights fight).
- **Outcome / status:** KIUC **dropped the stream-diversion component Dec 2023**; **March 2024
  settlement** — KIUC **withdrew the EA/FONSI** and agreed to hold a **public hearing** on any future
  environmental review; community groups dropped the suit. Project **scaled down** to the
  solar-powered pumped-storage system.
- **Delayed/blocked/killed:** **delayed / scaled-down.** Opposition removed the stream-diversion
  element; the PV component survives. **The resistance targeted the hydro/water component, never the
  solar.**
- **Verification:** VERIFIED (Earthjustice releases + West Kauai FEA responses-to-comments volume
  local; Civil Beat retained).
- Sources: https://earthjustice.org/press/2024/community-groups-reach-legal-settlement-with-kiuc-state-on-west-kauai-hydro-project ;

## STATEWIDE / LEGISLATIVE FORUM (resistance to permissive siting bills)

### 1. SB 631 (2011) — Sierra Club opposition to solar-on-ag-land liberalization
- **Group:** **Sierra Club Hawaiʻi Chapter** (Robert Harris, Anthony Aalto). **Forum:** State
  Legislature testimony. **Mechanism:** opposition testimony framed as food-security / anti-sprawl —
  solar as a "new and more expensive 'highest and best use'" raising ag-land prices. **Outcome:** the
  bill passed **with** the 10%/20-acre cap (Act 217, SLH 2011) *over* Sierra Club opposition; the
  Club's own wrap-up called it a "bad bill that passed." Venue-specific (ag-district integrity), same
  personnel/frame as its Koa Ridge/Hoʻopili anti-sprawl litigation — **not astroturf** (the Club was
  adversarial to the largest landholders). **Unaffected** (bill passed). VERIFIED via
  `notes/sierra-club-food-security.md`; 2011 testimony re-pull UNVERIFIED (capitol.hawaii.gov blocks
  scripts).

### 2. HB 593 (2019) — coalition opposition to solar on class-A prime ag land
- **Groups:** Dept of Agriculture; Office of Planning; **Hawaii Farm Bureau**; **Sierra Club**;
  **Life of the Land**. **Forum:** State Legislature testimony. **Mechanism:** coalition opposition
  to putting solar on A-rated prime ag land. **Outcome:** stiff opposition; the bill did not become
  law — an ag-policy fight, not a neighborhood one. Parallels the Hoʻohana boundary-amendment ag-land
  fight (2019, same coalition). VERIFIED (Star-Advertiser).
  Source: https://www.staradvertiser.com/2019/03/25/hawaii-news/bill-to-put-solar-farms-on-prime-ag-land-elicits-stiff-opposition/
