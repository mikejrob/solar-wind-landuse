# Legal representation map: contested Hawaii renewable siting cases

**Thread:** Who lawyers for whom in PUC PPA dockets, LUC special-use-permit dockets, and
related court appeals — and whether intervenor counsel have utility / large-landholder /
LURF ties (astroturf hypothesis test via revealed legal representation).

**Compiled:** 2026-07-11. Companion data: `data/legal_edges.csv`. Cached PDFs:
`data/raw/dockets/` (opinions, LUC D&Os, staff reports, Kahana settlement, lobbyist CSV).

**Evidentiary standard:** counsel names below come from opinion counsel blocks, signature
blocks, and LUC decisions & orders unless marked UNVERIFIED. Firm attributions inferred
only from name matches are flagged.

---

## Bottom line (preview)

The astroturf-via-lawyers hypothesis comes up **NULL** in every case examined. Community
intervenors against solar/wind/biomass projects are represented by a small, recurring
public-interest bar (Lance D. Collins, Bianca K. Isaki, Ryan D. Hurley, Christina D. Lizzi,
Chase H. Livingston) — solo/small practices with **no observed utility, landholder, or LURF
clients and no lobbyist registrations for such principals**. The mirror finding is the
stronger one: the traditional large-landowner land-use bar (Matsubara Kotake & Tabata,
Carlsmith Ball, McCorriston, Belles Graham) appears **FOR** renewable applicants, and legacy
landholders (Ulupalakua Ranch, Maui Land & Pineapple, Robinson Family Partners/Gay &
Robinson, Monsanto/Hartung as lessors, Kamehameha Schools) appear as project **lessors**. None
appears in opposition — consistent with the scarcity-rent framing (landholders monetize siting
rights) and inconsistent with landholder-funded opposition.

## PUC dockets

### Docket 2018-0433 — Paeahu Solar PPA (Maui Electric / Innergex), appeal SCOT-21-0000041

- Project: 15 MW solar + 60 MWh BESS, ~150 ac on **Ulupalakua Ranch** land above Maui
  Meadows, South Maui. PUC approved PPA Oct 2020 after contested proceeding; Supreme Court
  affirmed 2022-03-02 (Eddins, J.; Wilson, J. dissenting). PPA later terminated (Civil Beat).
- **Pono Power Coalition** (community group, Maui Meadows/Kihei residents) — granted
  participant status on constitutional clean-and-healthful-environment grounds.
  - Counsel of record: **Lance D. Collins** (Law Office of Lance D Collins, Wailuku);
    **Bianca K. Isaki** on the briefs. Source: opinion counsel block
    (`scot-21-0000041.pdf`).
  - Notable argued issue: winning Stage-1 bidders' post-selection use of **common counsel**
    to negotiate non-price PPA terms (Pono Power's antitrust/"rule of reason" argument —
    rejected). NB for the project: the *procurement-coordination* issue is itself in the
    record.
- Maui Electric: **Joseph A. Stewart, Bruce Nakamura, Aaron R. Mun — Kobayashi Sugita &
  Goda** (HECO companies' standing appellate counsel; same firm for HELCO in Hu Honua).
- Paeahu Solar LLC: **Douglas A. Codiga, Mark F. Ito — Schlack Ito LLLC** (same firm for
  Innergex's Kahana Solar).
- PUC: Ashley K.L. Agcaoili, Caroline C. Ishida, Mark J. Kaetsu.

### Paeahu county track — Maui Planning Commission SM permit contested case (2021-22)

- Intervenors **Pono Power Coalition + Maui Meadows Neighborhood Association** challenging

### Docket 2020-0142 — Kahana Solar PPA (Maui Electric / Innergex)

## Cross-cutting: firms appearing on multiple sides / multiple matters

1. **Matsubara Kotake & Tabata** — THE cross-link. Counsel for AES's solar SUPs (SP21-411,
   SP26-416, SP26-417) and long-time counsel for **Castle & Cooke** in LUC urban
   reclassification (Koa Ridge, A07-775; Sierra Club v. Castle & Cooke, ICA 2012: Benjamin
   M. Matsubara, Curtis T. Tabata of record). The large-landholder land-use bar works FOR
   utility-scale solar. It never appeared for its opponents in anything found.
2. **Kobayashi Sugita & Goda** — HECO companies in both contested PPA appeals (Paeahu,
   Hu Honua × 2). Utility-side only. (Joseph Stewart had one unrelated lobbyist
   registration: Airbnb, 2019-20.)
3. **Schlack Ito (Codiga/Ito)** — Innergex's counsel in both contested Maui solar PPAs.
   Developer-side energy regulatory bar.
4. **Yamamoto Caliboso lawyers** — Na Pua Makani (wind, CAAP-21-0000396) and Hu Honua
   (biomass, SCOT-22-0000418, Jesse Smith on both): the IPP-developer defense bar.
5. **Carlsmith Ball** — Na Pua Makani (AES wind) in the Supreme Court; separately, Carlsmith
   partner Gerald Sumida registered as lobbyist for **Ulupono Initiative**; Watanabe Ing's
   J. Douglas Ing likewise for Ulupono. Establishment firms sit on the pro-renewables side.
6. **Community intervenor bar** — Collins and Isaki are the connective tissue across
   solar (Paeahu), wind (KNSC ×2), and biomass (LOL): the *same two lawyers*, plus Hurley
   (Kahana/WMPA), Lizzi (Paeahu county track), Livingston (LOL 2023). Collins and Isaki
   also co-author the West Maui social-history book series (UH-affiliated) — an
   intellectual/activist network. It is not a commercial client network.

## Explicit nulls (publishable)

1. **No intervenor counsel in any examined docket has an identified utility, large-
   landholder, or LURF client relationship, past or present.** The opposition bar is
   public-interest/solo (Collins: Filipino community center, Front Street tenants,
   Native Hawaiian land cases, Rizal award; Isaki: KAHEA treasurer, PhD, pro bono
   environmental-review cases; Hurley: ex-PUC staff counsel; Lizzi: ex-deputy prosecutor;
   Livingston: plaintiff-side PI firm).
2. **LURF appears nowhere** in these dockets — no party, amicus, or counsel role in any
   renewable siting case found. LURF's revealed conduct is legislative (lobbyist
   registrations, testimony) — not resourcing project-level opposition.
3. **No large landholder appeared in opposition to any renewable project.** Where they
   appear at all, they are lessors/hosts (Ulupalakua Ranch, ML&P, Robinson/Gay & Robinson,
   Monsanto/Hartung/Fat Law's, and per prior verified work, Kamehameha Schools at
   Kawailoa/Waiawa).
4. **HECO appears only defending its own PPAs** (and testifying FOR Mahi Solar's SUP
   extension). No trace of the utility resourcing opposition to IPP projects. The
   Averch-Johnson/rate-base hypothesis gets no support from the legal-representation
   record; whatever siting friction exists here was not lawyer-supplied by HECO.
5. One community fight (Kahana/WMPA) ended in a **mediated community-benefits agreement**,
   and the Paeahu record shows Innergex made design changes — consistent with organic,
   negotiable local opposition. It was not scripted obstruction.
