## 2. Alexander & Baldwin: the standing bridge (and the "historically intertwined" question)

Verified shared directors (from HEI's own proxies):
- Constance Lau: A&B director + audit cttee 2004–2012 while HEI CEO (from 2006).
- Jeffrey Watanabe: A&B director 2003–2015, Lead Independent Director 2012–15,
  while HEI CHAIRMAN (2006–19).
- Thomas Fargo: A&B director ~2010–2012 (proxies inconsistent: 2012 proxy
  "since 2011"; 2015 proxy "2011-12"; 2019 proxy "2010-2011") while HEI and
  HECO director; later HEI board chair 2020–26.
- Shelee Kimura (HECO President & CEO): A&B director 2023–2026.
- Eric Yeaman (former HEI CFO): A&B director since June 2012 (A&B 8-K,
  sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/3453/000104746912006542/a2209861zex-99_1.htm);
  named KS trustee 2026. (URL: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/3453/000104746912006542/a2209861zex-99_1.htm)

During the 2011 legislative session (SB 631), THREE sitting HEI directors —
including the CEO and the board chairman — simultaneously sat on the board of
A&B, then Hawaii's largest publicly traded agricultural landowner (~87k
acres). After the 2012 A&B/Matson split, Lau and Fargo followed Matson
(shipping); Watanabe held both A&B and Matson seats to 2015/2018.

"Historically intertwined" (pre-2000): the one pre-2000 anchor verified so far
is C. Dudley Pratt Jr., HECO president & CEO 1981–1990, who served as a
trustee of the ESTATE OF JAMES CAMPBELL until year-end 1997 (Star-Bulletin
1997-08-26, https://archives.starbulletin.com/97/08/26/news/story3.html). Broader Big
Five–HECO directorate history NOT yet checked against pre-2001 proxies —
treat any stronger "historically intertwined" phrasing as UNVERIFIED pending
that check.

## 4. Parker Ranch, Grove Farm, Campbell, Damon: the current board

- Timothy E. Johns: HECO director since 2005, HECO audit chair since 2010,
  HECO board CHAIR since 2020, and HEI director nominee 2026 (boards merging
  after PUC D&O 42409, 2026-03-23, suspended affiliate separation rules) —
  simultaneously trustee of PARKER RANCH Foundation Trust/Parker Ranch Inc.
  since 2005 and director of GROVE FARM since 2004; formerly COO of the
  ESTATE OF SAMUEL MILLS DAMON, chair of the state Board of Land and Natural
  Resources, and VP/GC of Amfac Property Development 1994–98. (HEI 2026 proxy
  p.7. NOTE: proxy dates his DLNR directorship 2007-2011, which collides with
  his Bishop Museum CEO years in the same bio; BLNR service is usually dated
  ~1997-99 — date flagged UNVERIFIED.) Johns was on the HECO board throughout
  the 2011 SB 631 session.
- Micah Kāne: HEI director since 2019, became PRESIDENT & CEO OF PARKER RANCH
  effective 9/1/2025 while remaining an HEI director (parkerranch.com
  2025-02; HEI 2026 proxy p.8). So as of the 2026 annual meeting, the HEI
  board seats Parker Ranch's CEO and a 20-year Parker Ranch trustee (Johns).
- Richard J. Dahl: HEI director since 2017; President & CEO of JAMES CAMPBELL
  COMPANY LLC 2010–16 and non-executive chairman 2016–19 (2 years overlapping
  his HEI directorship); earlier Dole Food Co. president/COO 2002–07. (2019
  and 2023 proxies.) Predecessor pattern: Richard Gushman II, HEI director
  2007–2010, was simultaneously a James Campbell Co. director and DHHL/OHA
  advisory board member (2010 proxy).
- Kelvin Taketa: HEI director 1993–~2018; Grove Farm director (current in 2006
  proxy; "prior service" by 2010).
